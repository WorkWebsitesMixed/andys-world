This is the session that defines the next eighteen months of your D&T life. By the time you leave today you will know exactly what you are building, who you are building it for, and why those decisions matter for the IGCSE assessment. Everything from here until the final submission in Term 3 flows from the brief you write today.

<Section title="The Component 2 brief" icon="📋" id="brief">

### Four fixed constraints — everything else is your decision

Every student in this class is making the same type of product, but no two products will look the same. The four **fixed constraints** that apply to everyone:

1. The product must be a **load-bearing structural product** — a shelf, organiser, display stand, storage unit, or similar.
2. It must support a **minimum load of 5 kg** without permanent deformation.
3. It must include a **switch-operated light circuit** — a toggle switch, push button, or tilt switch controlling an LED or lamp.
4. It must use **at least two different structural joining methods** — for example adhesive plus a screwed joint, or a notched joint plus a KD fitting.

Everything else is your design decision: form, material, target user, aesthetic, colour, and specific function.

### The seven criteria (C1–C7)

| Criterion | What it covers | When |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Design brief + user profile | T1 W1 |
| C2 | Research + specification | T1 W8 |
| C3 | Idea generation + development | T1 W10 – T2 W2 |
| C4 | Modelling + testing | T2 W4–W6 |
| C5 | Planning + working drawings | T2 W9 – T3 W1 |
| C6 | Manufacture | T3 W2–W8 |
| C7 | Testing + evaluation | T3 W9 |

<WorkedExample title="Three radically different briefs — same constraints, different users">

**User A — Maya, 16, musician:** Guitar cables, picks, capo, and phone charger live in a pile on her desk. She needs an organised storage system at desk height with a built-in light for practising at night.

**Brief A:** Design a wall-mounted desk organiser for a teenage guitarist that holds at least four guitar accessories, supports a phone-charging cable without tangling, and includes a switch-operated LED to illuminate the workspace without glare. The product must support 5 kg.

---

**User B — Carlos, 15, cyclist:** Cycling helmet, gloves, lights, and repair kit pile up by the front door. He needs a hook-and-shelf unit that keeps gear accessible.

**Brief B:** Design a freestanding entry-hall organiser for a teenage cyclist that stores a helmet (mass approximately 350 g), gloves, and bike tools, with a switch-operated indicator LED to confirm the space is occupied. The product must support 5 kg total load.

---

All three constraints are met. The products look nothing alike.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Person profile and design brief" time="90 min" grouping="Individual (interview in pairs first 20 min)" materials={["A4 paper", "Pen", "Small card for term target"]}>

1. **(0–20 min) Interview a classmate.** Pair with someone you do not normally sit with. Interview them for 8 minutes using these prompts, then swap:
   - What do you store or organise that currently has no good home?
   - Where in your bedroom or workspace would a storage product go?
   - Do you use that space at night? Would a light help?
   - What specific items need storing, and how heavy are they?

2. **(20–50 min) Write a person profile.** On one A4 sheet: name (or pseudonym), age, context (where the product lives), two or three problems they face, and one direct quote from the interview.

3. **(50–80 min) Write a design brief.** Structure:
   - One sentence: who the user is and what the product is.
   - Three or more function points — each beginning "The product must..." and each testable.
   - One user-specific constraint beyond the four fixed ones.

4. **(80–90 min) Term target card.** Write one personal learning goal for Term 1. Make it specific: "I will annotate every sketch with material, join, and function before calling it done."
</Activity>

<Support title="Sentence starters for the brief">

**Opening:** "Design a [product type] for [user] who [context]."

**Function points:** "The product must [verb] [specific thing] [measurable if possible]."
- "The product must hold five textbooks (total mass approximately 4 kg) upright and accessible."
- "The product must include a switch-operated LED that illuminates the workspace without glare."

**Mistake to avoid:** Do not describe the form. "Made of wood with natural finish" is a design decision, not a function point. The brief states what it must *do*, not what it must *look like*.
</Support>
