Section A of Paper 4 asks short, direct questions across the whole syllabus: one-mark, two-mark, and three-mark items covering electronics, structures, mechanisms, materials, and sustainability. This bonus session is a full timed drill — 20 questions, 40 minutes — followed by a detailed topic-by-topic analysis.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  <ExamLink to="paper4">Paper 4</ExamLink> Section A is worth approximately 30 marks and covers the broadest range of topics. Unlike Section B (which is project-based and contextual), Section A tests recall directly. Drilling short-answer questions now identifies knowledge gaps while there is still time to address them before November.
</Callout>

### Five topic areas and key facts to know

**Electronics (8–10 marks per paper)**
- Ohm's Law: V = IR; resistor calculation for LED circuit
- Series vs parallel circuits: current and voltage behaviour
- Component symbols: resistor, LED, switch, battery, transistor, LDR, thermistor
- Transistor as a switch: base, collector, emitter; threshold voltage behaviour

**Structures (6–8 marks per paper)**
- Forces: tension, compression, shear, torsion — define each with an example
- Triangulation: why triangles are rigid (cannot deform without changing side length)
- Beam deflection: where the maximum deflection occurs in a simply supported beam
- Factor of safety: why structures are designed to carry more than expected load

**Mechanisms (4–6 marks per paper)**
- Gear ratio: driven/driver or driver/driven — output speed vs torque
- Cam types: eccentric, snail, pear-shaped — output motion of each
- Linkage types: parallel linkage, bell-crank — change of direction

**Materials (4–6 marks per paper)**
- Hardwood vs softwood: what the terms mean (not always hardness)
- Material properties: ductile, malleable, brittle, tough — with examples
- Composite materials: why fibres in a matrix outperform either constituent alone

**Sustainability (2–4 marks per paper)**
- 6Rs: Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Repair, Rethink — a specific example for each
- Carbon footprint and embedded energy
- Life cycle assessment: extraction, manufacturing, use, disposal

</Section>

<Activity title="20-question timed drill + topic analysis" time="45 min" grouping="Individual — exam conditions for questions, then open discussion" materials={["Question set (teacher issued)", "Calculator", "Topic score sheet"]}>

**Timed drill (0–40 min):**
1. Teacher issues 20 questions — 4 from each topic area.
2. 2 minutes per question average. Work in silence.
3. No notes, no textbook.

**Self-mark and topic analysis (40–45 min):**
4. Teacher issues answers. Mark your own responses.
5. On the topic score sheet: record marks per topic (out of 4 each).
6. Circle the lowest-scoring topic: this is your August revision priority 1.
7. Circle the second-lowest: this is priority 2.
</Activity>

<Support title="Multiple topics scoring low?">

**Three or more topics under 2/4?** This is not unusual at this stage in Term 3 — you have been making, not revising theory. The August revision block is specifically designed for this gap. Today's drill tells you where to start. Use the topic list above as a structured syllabus.

**Mechanisms specifically difficult?** Mechanisms (gears, cams, linkages) has the least overlap with the project brief. If you scored 0–1/4, plan two dedicated mechanisms revision sessions in August: Collins §5.2, worked examples, and then past paper questions for that topic only.
</Support>
