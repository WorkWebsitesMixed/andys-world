<Section title="Pre-exam technique review" icon="🎯" id="technique">

### Command words: what each requires

| Command word | What to write | Typical marks |
|---|---|---|
| State | One specific fact. No explanation. | 1 |
| Identify | Name or label the item. | 1 |
| Describe | What it looks like, what it does, how it works — no reason needed. | 1–2 |
| Explain | State the fact AND give the reason. "X is used because Y." | 2–3 |
| Calculate | Show formula, units, substitution, result, unit. | 2–4 |
| Suggest | Propose a specific example. Justification needed for multi-mark. | 2–3 |
| Justify | Give specific evidence-based reasons for a choice made. | 2–4 |
| Evaluate | Make a judgement (good/bad, better/worse) with evidence for and against. | 4–6 |

### First-pass / second-pass strategy

1. **First pass (Section A):** Answer every question you know immediately. Skip any question that requires thought. Mark skipped questions with a tick.
2. **Second pass (Section A):** Return to ticked questions. Attempt each — even a partial answer. Never leave blank.
3. **Section B:** Read all sub-parts before answering any. Margin plan for 4+ mark questions. Answer in order.

</Section>

<Section title="The ten most common error types in Paper 4" icon="⚠️" id="errors">

1. **Unit not stated after calculation.** "350" instead of "350 Ω" — often costs the final mark.
2. **mA substituted as A** in the resistor formula or Ohm's Law.
3. **Generic vocabulary** — "strong", "light", "durable" without values.
4. **Beam reaction formula inverted** — RA calculated as if closer to load, not farther.
5. **Wrong FoS direction** — dividing working load by ultimate instead of the reverse.
6. **Section B answers not linked to context** — true statement, but not about THIS design.
7. **Thermistor/LDR position confused** — top vs bottom in voltage divider for the required response.
8. **First-angle projection side view on the wrong side** — plan view above instead of below.
9. **Specification points not measurable** — "it must look good" type non-testable criteria.
10. **Evaluation without evidence** — "the product met the specification" without any test result.

Study this list. Identify which of the ten you are most likely to commit. Write it on the inside cover of your exam paper before starting.

</Section>

<Activity title="Graded M6 mixed past paper" time="90 min" grouping="Individual (exam conditions)" materials={["M6 past paper (teacher-provided)", "Calculator", "Black pen", "Pencil for sketches", "Ruler"]}>

**Before starting (3 min):**
1. Write your personal error type from the list of ten on the inside front cover of the paper.
2. Read the Section B context before answering Section A.

**Section A (40 min):**
3. First pass: answer all questions you know immediately.
4. Second pass: return to skipped questions. Attempt every question.

**Section B (40 min):**
5. Read all sub-parts of each Section B question before answering.
6. Margin plan for any question worth 4 marks or more.
7. Link all answers to the Section B design context.

**Check-back (7 min):**
8. Check: sub-parts all answered? Units on all calculations? No blanks?

After the paper is marked (next session): compare score to M5. Identify which of the ten error types you committed and which you successfully avoided.
</Activity>

<Support title="In-exam support techniques">

**Question feels impossible:** Write what you know about the topic — even if you cannot answer the specific question directly. A definition of the relevant term, a related fact, or a formula may earn partial marks.

**Running out of time:** In the final 3 minutes, write one key phrase for any unanswered question: the relevant term, a formula, or a property name. This can earn 1 mark per question — multiple 1-mark recoveries matter.

**Cannot remember a value:** Use a comparative statement: "Aluminium is less dense than mild steel" earns the ORA mark even without stating 2.7 g/cm³. "A higher FoS is needed in safety-critical applications" earns marks even if the specific FoS value is forgotten.
</Support>
