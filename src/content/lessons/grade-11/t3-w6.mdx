The structure is assembled and the circuit is tested. Now the two come together permanently. Today you fix the electronic components into their final positions, route all wiring cleanly, and apply the first coat of surface finish. The product is almost complete.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">

The difference between a 20/30 and a 28/30 in C6 is usually in the finishing — clean surfaces, consistent colouring, neat wiring. Photographs at this stage are evidence for those marks.
</Callout>

<Section title="Finishing and electronics integration" icon="✨" id="finishing">

### Surface preparation sequence

1. **120 grit:** sand all external faces, following the grain. Remove all tool marks and rough areas.
2. **180 grit:** re-sand all faces. The surface should feel smooth to the touch.
3. **240 grit:** final pass. Remove any remaining scratches from the 180-grit stage.
4. **Dust removal:** use a tack cloth or clean dry brush. No dust should remain in the grain.
5. **Apply finish coat 1:** thin coat, following manufacturer instructions. Work the finish into the grain with the cloth.

### Electronics integration

1. Fix the circuit board permanently into the bracket using M2 screws or hot glue.
2. Route all wires through channels. Secure at intermediate points with cable clips or hot glue blobs.
3. Apply strain relief at the switch connection and at the battery holder wires.
4. Fix the battery holder in the base cavity. Ensure the battery can be replaced without disassembling the product.
5. Final function test: switch on, LED lights, switch off, LED off. Photograph.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Finishing + integration for a cyclist gear organiser">

**Surface prep:**
- Sanded all faces: 120 → 180 → 240 grit. Grain direction followed on all panels. Top rail required extra 120-grit pass to remove saw marks at one end.
- Tack cloth applied. Surface felt smooth, no grit remaining.

**Finish coat 1:**
- Hardwax oil applied with lint-free cloth. Thin coat, worked into grain. Left 20 minutes. Buffed lightly. Surface shows a slight sheen — consistent across all faces. Good.

**Electronics:**
- Circuit board fixed in bracket with M2 × 6 screws.
- Power wires routed through base cable channel, secured with two hot-glue blobs.
- Switch secured with lock-nut in front panel hole.
- Battery holder fixed in base cavity — battery slides in and out without removing any panel.
- Strain relief: hot glue bead at switch wire entry.
- Function test passed. Photograph taken: LED lit, switch visible.
</WorkedExample>

**Surface preparation (0–30 min):**
1. Sand all external faces: 120, 180, 240 grit, in order. Follow the grain.
2. Remove all dust with tack cloth.

**Finish coat 1 (30–45 min):**
3. Apply a thin coat of finish to all external faces.
4. Leave to penetrate (check product instructions). Buff lightly.
5. Photograph the finish at this stage.

**Electronics integration (45–80 min):**
6. Permanently fix the circuit board in the bracket.
7. Route and secure all wiring. Apply strain relief at connection points.
8. Fix battery holder. Test battery access.
9. Final function test. Photograph: LED lit, switch visible from user position.

10. Write the W6 making log entry: surface prep sequence, finish coat 1 status, electronics integration completed, function test result.

<Support title="Finish not going on evenly?">

**Blotchy oil finish?** Usually caused by dust or grease on the surface before application. Strip the coat (naphtha or mineral spirits on a cloth, follow ventilation rules) and re-sand from 180 grit. Ensure no fingerprints on the surface before applying the finish.

**Oil soaking in too fast on end grain?** End grain is more porous than face grain and absorbs finish faster, leaving a darker or patchy appearance. Apply a thin sealing coat to end grain first (rubbed in with fingertip), leave to set for 5 minutes, then apply the main coat. This reduces over-absorption.

**Wire too short to reach the switch?** Extend with a splice: strip both ends, twist together, solder, cover with heat-shrink tubing. A soldered and heat-shrunk splice is permanent and clean. Twisted-only connections are not acceptable — they corrode and fail.
</Support>
