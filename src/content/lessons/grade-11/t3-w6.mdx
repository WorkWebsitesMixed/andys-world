The difference between "made" and "well-made" is the **finish**. This week you smooth,
seal and protect your product, and work through your **snag list** — the small jobs
that make it look and feel professional.

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Why finish at all?

A finish does three jobs: **protection** (against wear and moisture), **aesthetics**
(it looks intentional), and **safety** (smooth, deburred edges). An unfinished product
loses marks even if the structure is good.

### 2. Match the finish to the material

- **Wood** — sand smooth, then seal or oil/varnish.
- **Metal** — deburr edges, then protect against corrosion.
- **Plastic** — clean cut edges; polish acrylic if needed.

]} />

### 3. Work your snag list

Write down every small flaw and loose end, then fix them one by one — a wobbly hook, a
rough edge, a missing fixing.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — finishing a timber frame">
1. **Sand** the timber smooth, working through finer grits.
2. **Deburr** any sharp cut edges so it's safe to handle.
3. **Seal** the surface (oil or varnish) for protection and looks.
4. **Snag check:** tighten the loose hook and replace the missing screw.
</WorkedExample>
