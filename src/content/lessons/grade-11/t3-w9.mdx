<Section title="The five essential formulas" icon="🔢" id="formulas">

Write each from memory before looking at this page. Then check.

**Formula 1 — Current-limiting resistor:**
R = (Vsupply - V_LED) / I_LED
[Convert I_LED from mA to A before substituting]

**Formula 2 — Voltage divider:**
Vout = Vsupply x R2 / (R1 + R2)
[R1 = top; R2 = bottom. Vout must be between 0 V and Vsupply]

**Formula 3 — Ohm's Law:**
V = I x R (rearranges to I = V / R and R = V / I)

**Formula 4 — Factor of Safety:**
FoS = Ultimate load / Working load
[FoS > 1.0 for safe design. FoS = 2.0 means twice the working load before failure]

**Formula 5 — Stress:**
Stress = Force / Area
[Force in N, Area in mm², Result in N/mm² = MPa]

**Bonus formula — Beam reaction:**
RA = W x (distance from load to B) / span
[Check: RA + RB = W]

</Section>

<Section title="Pre-exam protocol — the exact sequence" icon="🎯" id="protocol">

### Five minutes at the start of every Paper 4 exam

1. Write candidate number and name.
2. Write formulas in the margin: R, Vout, FoS, Stress, RA.
3. Write personal error list on inside cover: mA → A, top/bottom divider, beam reaction direction, unit after calculation.
4. Read Section B context.
5. Note marks per section. Allocate: Section A = 40 minutes, Section B = 75 minutes, review = 5 minutes.
6. Begin Section A, first pass.

This protocol should take exactly 5 minutes. Practise it today — time yourself.

### Five minutes at the end of every Paper 4 exam

1. Check: every sub-part answered?
2. Check: unit stated after every calculation?
3. Check: any complete blanks? Write a partial attempt for each.
4. Stop.

</Section>

<Section title="Open Q&A — common end-of-year questions" icon="💬" id="qa">

### "Which topics come up most often in Paper 4?"

Structures (types, forces, FoS, stress, beam reactions) and materials (properties, justification) appear in Section A every year. Electronics (IPO, resistor, voltage divider) appears in 80%+ of recent papers. Sustainability (6Rs) appears in Section A and sometimes Section B. Orthographic projection and specification writing appear occasionally in Section B.

### "What is the hardest question in Section A?"

Beam reaction calculations with two point loads (not covered in depth this year — see bonus sessions). Composite material property questions that require comparing two properties simultaneously. Voltage divider with the sensor in an unusual position.

### "What is the most reliable source of marks in Section B?"

An explain-and-justify question with 4–6 marks on materials selection — if you apply the three-part format (property + value + consequence for this context), full marks are consistently available.

### "How much does my coursework count?"

Component 2 coursework (the project) is worth 60% of the IGCSE D&T grade. Paper 1 (design communication) is 20%. Paper 4 (Section A + B) is 20%. Your project grade is set — the remaining papers determine where you fall within your coursework band.

</Section>

<Activity title="Formula drill, Q&A, and independent revision plan" time="90 min" grouping="Individual and class" materials={["Formula card (blank — write it yourself)", "Independent revision plan template (teacher-provided)", "Any remaining exam questions for individual Q&A"]}>

**Formula recall drill (0–15 min):**
1. Write all five formulas (plus beam reaction) from memory in 90 seconds. No notes.
2. Check against this page. For any formula that was wrong or missing: write it three times from memory with a 30-second gap between each writing.

**Timed pre-exam protocol (15–25 min):**
3. On a blank sheet of paper, run through the five-minute pre-exam protocol exactly as it will happen in the exam room. Time yourself. It should take 5 minutes or less.

**Open Q&A (25–55 min):**
4. Students raise questions. Any topic from T1–T3. Teacher responds to questions as raised. Write down any answer that resolves a question you had.

**Independent revision plan (55–80 min):**
5. Complete the independent revision plan template:
   - My two weakest topics from M6: [topic 1] and [topic 2].
   - Revision activity for topic 1: [specific — e.g. "Complete 15 structures Section A questions per day, self-mark"].
   - Revision activity for topic 2: [specific].
   - Formula drill schedule: ["Write all formulas from memory, morning of each revision day"].
   - Full timed past paper dates: [two dates before the exam, with exam conditions — alone, timed, no notes].
   - Final review: ["Day before exam: formula recall + personal error list review. No new material."]

**Send-off (80–90 min):**
6. Share your independent revision plan with the teacher for feedback.
7. Final reflection: write one sentence — what are you most confident about, and one sentence — what are you still working on.
</Activity>
