<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Why CAD?

CAD gives you **accuracy, easy editing, sharing, and a direct link to manufacture**.
Change one dimension and the whole part updates — then the same file cuts the part.

### 2. Vector vs raster

The laser **cuts along vectors** (lines/paths) and **engraves rasters** (images).
For parts you want cut out, everything must be **vector**.

### 3. The laser-cut workflow

- Separate **cut** lines from **engrave** areas (usually by colour or layer).
- Allow for **kerf** — the laser removes a sliver of material, so tight-fitting
  slots need adjusting.
- Get the **material settings** right (power/speed), and follow safety: fume
  extraction on, **never leave the machine unattended**.

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a laser-ready bracket plate">
1. **Draw** a 100 × 60 mm plate in CAD.
2. **Add** two fixing holes, dimensioned from an edge.
3. **Assign layers:** outline + holes on the **cut** layer; a label on the **engrave** layer.
4. **Export** the file ready for the cutter.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="CAD your first part" time="50 min" grouping="Paired workstations" materials={["CAD workstation", "Step-by-step CAD handout", "Sample sheet stock"]}>
