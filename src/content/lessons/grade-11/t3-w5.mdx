Time to bring your product to life. This week you fit the **circuit** you tested on a
breadboard into the real product — mounting it neatly, securing the wiring, and
confirming the light works once everything is together.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  The working circuit is a **required feature** of <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 6</ExamLink>. A neatly mounted, reliably working light is part of "functions well" — and
  it's what makes your product feel finished.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Mounting", definition: "Fixing components (switch, LED, battery) securely into the product." },

  { term: "Switch placement", definition: "Putting the switch where your user can reach it easily." },
  { term: "In-situ testing", definition: "Testing the circuit after it's fitted, not just on the bench." },
]} />

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Mount it securely

Fix the **switch, LED and battery** so they won't rattle loose. Think about **switch
placement** — it should be where *your user* naturally reaches.

<CircuitDiagram />

<WatchSee items={[
  { label: "Soldering 101 — a beginner's guide", href: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rK38rpUy568", kind: "Video", note: "How to make a clean, safe soldered joint (if you solder your circuit)." }
]} />

### 2. Protect the wiring

Add **strain relief** so the wires aren't pulled at their connections (a small clip or
loop). Loose wiring is the most common reason a working circuit later fails.

### 3. Test in situ

A circuit that worked on the breadboard can still fail once squeezed into a product.
**Test it in situ** — switch on, light on — and fix any fault now.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — neat, reliable integration">
1. **Mount the switch** where the user reaches it; mount the battery accessibly.
2. **Route the wiring** tidily inside the frame, away from moving or sharp parts.
3. **Add strain relief** so no wire is pulled at its joint.
4. **Test in situ:** switch on — the light works. Done right.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Fit and wire your circuit" time="Full session (technician supervises)" grouping="Staggered stations" materials={["Your circuit kit", "Supervised soldering (if used)", "Wiring & clips"]}>
1. **Mount and wire** the circuit into your product.
2. **Secure the wiring** with strain relief.
3. **Test that the light works** in situ.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? Solderless options & supervision">
You can use **solderless connectors** and **pre-tinned leads** — no soldering needed.
If soldering, the **technician supervises or does it** with you. Always ask before you
act if you're unsure.
</Support>
