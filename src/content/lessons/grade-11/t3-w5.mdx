<Section title="Wiring and circuit integration" icon="⚡" id="wiring">

### Soldering the circuit

If your circuit is to be soldered onto stripboard (not just breadboarded):

1. Plan the layout on the stripboard first — draw the component positions on paper.
2. Insert components from the component side (smooth face), bend leads to hold in place.
3. Solder from the copper track side: heat the joint (not the solder) for 2 seconds, feed solder, remove solder, remove iron.
4. Inspect each joint: shiny, slightly convex, forms a clean fillet around the lead.
5. Test continuity: beep across each connection. Test function: switch on — LED lights.

### Integrating the circuit into the product

1. Mount the circuit board in the bracket. Route wires through the cable channel.
2. Fix the switch in its designed position (recess, bezel, or drilled hole depending on your design).
3. Fit the LED in its designed position (top of frame, recess in panel, etc.).
4. Fix the battery holder in the base cavity. Route the power wires.
5. Final test: switch on. LED lights. Switch off. LED extinguishes. Both states confirmed.

</Section>

<Support title="Soldering problems?">

**Cold joint?** The most common cause is insufficient heat time. Heat the joint (the component lead and the copper track) for 2 full seconds before introducing solder. Do not heat the solder — heat the joint, then touch solder to the joint (not to the iron). The solder should flow onto the hot joint.

**Solder bridging between tracks?** Use solder wick (desoldering braid) to absorb excess. Place the wick over the bridge, press with a hot iron. The wick wicks up the excess solder. If no wick available, a continuity test will confirm whether the bridge is causing a short.

**LED not lighting after integration?** Check polarity: the long leg (anode) connects to the positive power supply side. If polarity is correct, check that the switch is connecting (continuity test across the switch). If the switch is open-circuit when 'on', it is wired backwards — reverse the switch connections.
</Support>
