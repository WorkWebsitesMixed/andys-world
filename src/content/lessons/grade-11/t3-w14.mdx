A full additional Paper 1 timed practice with a focus on the second half of the paper: parts (d) through (g). These parts often lose marks because of time pressure in the main session — this bonus provides extra repetition in a shorter format.

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Part (d) development: before and after", definition: "Part (d) requires a development sketch — not a new idea from scratch. The development must: (a) reference a specific aspect of one of the part (c) ideas (e.g. 'the joint between the shelf and the side panel in Idea 2'), (b) show the original version (before), (c) show the proposed change (after), and (d) explain in one sentence how the change improves the design." },
  { term: "Part (e) sustainability response structure", definition: "For a 4-mark part (e) sustainability question: name two different Rs, describe the design feature for each (specific to the context), and explain the sustainability benefit. Two Rs x 2 marks each = 4 marks. Never name the R without the product-specific feature and benefit." },
  { term: "Part (g) evaluation language", definition: "Evaluation sentences must use explicit judgement language: 'met', 'partially met', or 'not met', followed by specific evidence from the designed product. Do not use hedging language ('might meet', 'could be suitable', 'seems to'). Make a clear decision." },
]} />

<Section title="Parts (d)–(g) revision guide" icon="📄" id="guide">

### Part (d) — Development [4 marks, 8 minutes]

Draw a before sketch (small, 5 cm x 5 cm) of the specific part being developed. Label: "Before: [state the problem]."
Draw an after sketch (same size) showing the change. Label: "After: [state the change]."
Write one sentence: "This change improves the design because [specific structural or functional reason linked to the context]."

Marks awarded for: identifying the aspect being changed (1), showing the change in sketch form (1), explaining why the change is an improvement (2).

### Part (e) — Sustainability [4 marks, 7 minutes]

Two Rs, each with:
- Named R.
- Design feature (specific to the design context).
- Sustainability benefit.

Format: "Repair: the product uses M5 machine screws to secure all panels, allowing individual components to be replaced if damaged — extending product life and reducing material disposal."

### Part (f) — Testing criteria [4 marks, 7 minutes]

Two criteria:
- State the criterion (from one of the part (a) function points or a specification point implied by the context).
- State the test method: what equipment, how measured, what constitutes a pass.

Format: "The product width must not exceed 380 mm: tested by measuring from the leftmost to rightmost point with a steel rule — result must be 380 mm or less."

### Part (g) — Evaluation [4 marks, 7 minutes]

For each function point from part (a): one judgement sentence. Mark schemes typically award 1 mark per function point addressed with evidence.

Format: "Function point 1 [store 5 pens upright]: MET — the cylindrical holder in Idea 2 has five 14 mm diameter slots, sufficient for standard pen diameters."

</Section>

<Activity title="Parts (d)–(g) timed practice" time="45 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["Part (c) sketches from T3 W4 simulation or a new context (teacher-provided)", "Pencil for sketches, black pen for written parts"]}>

**Context brief (2 min):**
Use the design context from the main session (T3 W4) or a new teacher-provided context. Reference your part (c) ideas for all subsequent parts.

**Part (d) — Development (8 min):**
1. Choose one specific aspect of one part (c) idea. Draw before and after. Write the improvement sentence.

**Part (e) — Sustainability (7 min):**
2. Write two sustainability points, each with R name, design feature, and sustainability benefit.

**Part (f) — Testing criteria (7 min):**
3. Write two testable criteria. Each must have a specific test method (equipment + procedure + pass criterion).

**Part (g) — Evaluation (7 min):**
4. For each function point from part (a): write one judgement sentence with evidence from your part (c) ideas.

**Self-mark (10 min):**
5. Mark scheme provided. Count distinct mark points for each part. For any mark missed: identify the exact missing component (was the R named? Was the test equipment specified? Was the judgement explicit?).

**Write three lessons learned (4 min):**
6. Three bullet points: the three most frequent mark-scheme points you missed and how to avoid them next time.
</Activity>
