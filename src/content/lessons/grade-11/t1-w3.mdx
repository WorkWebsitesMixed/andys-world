happening inside them: the **forces** trying to break them, and the **members**
that resist those forces. This is the heart of the Structures exam question.

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. The five forces

Every load puts one or more of these five forces into a structure:

- **Tension** — pulling apart (a tow rope).
- **Compression** — squeezing (a column under a roof).
- **Shear** — sliding/cutting (the bolt in a pair of scissors).
- **Bending** — a shelf sagging under books.
- **Torsion** — twisting (turning a screwdriver).

A clever tip: when something **bends**, the outer (stretched) face is in **tension**
and the inner (squeezed) face is in **compression** at the same time.

### 2. Members: tie, strut and beam

The parts of a frame are **members**, and we name them by the force they carry:

- a **tie** is in **tension** (pulled),
- a **strut** is in **compression** (squeezed),
- a **beam** resists **bending**.

How well a member does its job depends on its **length, cross-sectional shape and
material** — a deep, I-shaped steel beam resists bending far better than a thin flat
bar of the same weight.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a cantilever shelf">
1. **Picture it.** A shelf is fixed to a wall at one end, with a heavy plant pot on the free end.
2. **What does it do?** It **bends** downward.
3. **Top face?** It is stretched → in **tension**.
4. **Bottom face?** It is squeezed → in **compression**.

→ A loaded cantilever **bends**, with **tension on top and compression underneath**.
</WorkedExample>

<Support title="Need a hand? A colour key + sentence frame">
**Colour key** (use the same colours every time): tension = blue, compression =

  {
    q: "A crane's cable lifting a load is mainly in which force?",
    options: ["Compression", "Tension", "Torsion", "Shear"],
    answer: 1,
    hint: "Is the cable being pulled apart, or squeezed?",
    explain: "The load pulls the cable apart, so it is in tension."
  },
  {
    q: "A member that is being squeezed is called a…",
    options: ["tie", "strut", "beam", "joint"],
    answer: 1,
    hint: "Squeezing is compression — which member name goes with compression?",
    explain: "A strut is a member in compression. (A tie is in tension; a beam resists bending.)"
  },
  {
    q: "A diving board with a person standing on the end mainly experiences…",
    options: ["torsion", "shear", "bending", "tension only"],
    answer: 2,
    hint: "What shape does the board take when someone stands on the end?",
    explain: "The board bends — its top face goes into tension and its bottom face into compression."
  }
