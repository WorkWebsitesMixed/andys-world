This week you **resolve** your development — turning your test results into the
final design you'll plan and make — and you give it a **sustainability check** with
the 6Rs. After this, your development folder is ready to be marked.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This finishes <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 4</ExamLink> (development) and feeds
  sustainability into your spec and <ExamLink to="paper1">Paper 1 part (f)</ExamLink>.
  A resolved design means decisions made **from evidence**, not guesses.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Resolve from the evidence

A **resolved** development isn't your prettiest idea — it's the one your **tests
support**. Lock in the final form, materials and joints, and show *why* each choice
follows from a test result or a spec point.

### 2. Review with the 6Rs

Run your product through **Rethink, Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repair, Recycle**. Look
especially at **material efficiency** (use less) and **design for disassembly**
(can it be repaired or recycled?).

<WatchSee items={[
  { label: "What are the 6 Rs of sustainability?", href: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yY47lnKgmg4", kind: "Video", note: "A walk through Rethink, Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repair, Recycle." }
]} />

### 3. Trace every change

Each refinement should trace back to **a spec point and a test result**. That trail
is what earns development marks.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a refinement that's also greener">
1. **Test finding:** the cyclist organiser's glued joint can't be opened, so it can't be repaired.
2. **Refine:** swap the glued joint for a **knock-down (KD) fitting**.
3. **Result:** the organiser is now **repairable *and* recyclable** — a 6Rs win.
4. **Justify:** the change meets the "repairable" spec point *and* improves Reuse/Recycle.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Resolve and review" time="50 min" grouping="Individual, with a partner check" materials={["Your test-results pages", "6Rs prompt cards", "Your materials list"]}>
1. **Produce your resolved development outcome** — the design you'll plan and make.
2. **Run a 6Rs review** and record at least two sustainability improvements.
3. **Update** your specification and materials list to match.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? A 6Rs card + a development checklist">
Use the **6Rs prompt card** and pick the two Rs that fit your product best. Check
off the **development checklist** — resolved form ✓, materials decided ✓, joints
decided ✓, each change traced to evidence ✓.
</Support>
