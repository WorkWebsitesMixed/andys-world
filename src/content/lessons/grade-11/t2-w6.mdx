The structure is assembled. Two tasks remain before C6 is complete: surface finishing (which determines whether the product meets the aesthetics specification) and circuit installation (which determines whether it meets the electronics specification). Both are assessed together as part of the finished product quality.

<Section title="Surface finishing process" icon="🎨" id="finishing">

### Sequence for MDF finishing

1. **Sand bare MDF:** Start with 120-grit to remove machining marks, finish with 180-grit for a smooth surface. Sand with the direction of the face (MDF has no grain but sanding across the face can scuff the surface).
2. **Seal edges:** MDF edges are highly porous. Apply a coat of diluted PVA (1 part PVA : 3 parts water) or a dedicated MDF edge primer. Allow to dry fully (30–60 minutes).
3. **First paint coat:** Apply a water-based primer. Leave 30 minutes to dry. The first coat will raise fibres — the surface will feel rough when dry.
4. **Light sand:** 180-grit. Remove raised fibres without removing paint. Wipe dust.
5. **Second and third paint coats:** Apply topcoats. Each coat should be thin — several thin coats produce a better finish than one thick coat that runs.
6. **Finishing touches:** Sand lightly between coats if needed. Final coat should be left without sanding.

### Alternative finishes

| Finish | Process | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Spray paint | Rattle can or spray gun | Smooth, even coating; faster than brush |
| Acrylic paint (brush) | Water-based; multiple coats | Available in school workshop |
| Wood stain + varnish | Stain then seal with acrylic varnish | Natural wood appearance on pine/hardwood |
| Clear lacquer | Spray or brush | Protects without changing colour |

<Section title="Circuit installation" icon="⚡" id="circuit">

### Installation sequence

1. **Solder the circuit board** (if using strip board or prototype board): position components on the board, solder all joints, check continuity with a multimeter.
2. **Test the circuit on the board** before installing it: switch should close the circuit and light the LED. Switch open should extinguish the LED.
3. **Mount the switch** in its drilled hole in the product. Secure with the mounting nut. Do not overtighten — MDF will crack.
4. **Mount the LED** in its drilled hole. The longer leg (anode) connects to the resistor. Use a 3 mm LED holder if available to secure the LED without gluing.
5. **Route wires** through the wire channel. Leave 50 mm extra wire at each connection point for soldering. Secure with cable clips at 150 mm intervals.
6. **Connect battery clip to circuit.** Red wire to positive rail. Black wire to negative (0 V) rail.
7. **Final test:** Insert battery. Toggle the switch on — LED lights? Toggle off — LED extinguishes? Check that no components get unusually warm (indicates a wiring error or incorrect resistor).

### Soldering guide

- Tin the iron tip before each joint (apply a small amount of solder to a clean iron tip, wipe on a damp sponge).
- Heat the joint (component leg and pad together), not the solder.
- Apply solder to the joint, not to the iron.
- A good joint: shiny, conical, wets both the lead and the pad. 2–3 seconds total contact.
- A cold joint: dull, grainy, does not wet fully. Re-melt and add a small amount of fresh solder.

</Section>

**Surface preparation (0–25 min):**
1. Sand all external surfaces: 120-grit then 180-grit.
2. Seal MDF edges with diluted PVA or primer. Allow to dry.
3. Apply first paint coat to all surfaces. Set aside to dry.

**While paint dries — circuit preparation (25–50 min):**
4. Solder the resistor and LED in series on a strip board or solder directly to switch and LED legs (simplest approach for this project).
5. Test the circuit before installation (battery in, switch closed = LED on, switch open = LED off).
6. Drill mounting holes in the product for the switch (8 mm) and LED (3 mm) in the positions documented in C4.

**Circuit installation (50–70 min):**
7. Mount switch in its hole. Mount LED in its hole.
8. Route wires through the wire channel. Secure with cable clips.
9. Connect battery clip. Perform final installed test.

**Second paint coat and finish (70–90 min):**
10. Light sand between coats if the surface feels rough (grain raise).
11. Apply second paint coat.
12. Photograph: finished product from front, from side, circuit visible through open battery panel.

<Support title="Finishing and circuit troubleshooting">

**Paint uneven or blotchy on MDF?** The edges were not sealed. Diluted PVA seals the porous edge fibres — without it, the topcoat soaks in unevenly. Let the current coat dry, apply diluted PVA to all blotchy areas, allow to dry, then re-apply topcoat.

**LED does not light after installation?** Check in order: (1) battery charged/new? (2) switch mounted correctly (make sure the toggle is in the closed position)? (3) LED polarity correct (longer leg to resistor, not to negative)? (4) all solder joints shiny and well-wetted? A multimeter in continuity mode will identify any open break in the circuit.

**Switch wobbles in its hole?** The hole is too large for the switch body. Pack with epoxy putty around the thread and retighten the mounting nut when cured.
</Support>
