Back to your project. You've met your user and gathered research — now you turn that
into a **specification**: the precise, measurable list of what your product *must*
do. A strong spec is the backbone of every mark that follows.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This is <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 2</ExamLink> of your coursework
  (research → specification, 10 marks). A clear, justified spec also makes idea
  generation and evaluation far easier later — and the analysis skill transfers
  straight to Paper 1.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Primary research", definition: "Information you gather yourself (interviewing or observing your user)." },
  { term: "Secondary research", definition: "Information from existing sources (books, websites, existing products)." },
  { term: "Specification", definition: "A list of measurable, justified statements of what the product must do or be." },
  { term: "Measurable", definition: "Written so you can test it — a number, a limit or a clear yes/no." },
  { term: "ACCESS FM", definition: "A spec framework: Aesthetics, Cost, Customer, Environment, Size, Safety, Function, Materials." },
]} />

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Analyse, don't just collect

Research only earns marks when you **analyse** it — say what each finding *means for
your design*. "My user keeps heavy folders" → "the shelf must support at least 5 kg".

### 2. ACCESS FM — a checklist for a complete spec

Cover each heading so nothing is missed: **A**esthetics, **C**ost, **C**ustomer,
**E**nvironment, **S**ize, **S**afety, **F**unction, **M**aterials. Your make-palette
and the project's <ExamLink to="project">size limits</ExamLink> already fix some of
these for you.

### 3. What a good spec point looks like

Every point should be **measurable** *and* **justified**:

> *"The product must support 5 kg **because** the user stores heavy folders
> (**measured by** loading it with 5 kg without sagging)."*

Include at least one **safety** point and one **sustainability** point.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — turning research into spec points">
1. **Finding:** "My user's desk is only 60 cm deep and always cluttered."
2. **Spec point:** *"Must take up no more than 25 cm of desk depth, because the desk is small and cluttered."*
3. **Finding:** "They work late and the room is dark."
4. **Spec point:** *"Must include a switch-operated light, because the user works in low light."*

→ Each point traces back to something the **user actually said**.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Draft your specification" time="40 min" grouping="Individual, with a peer check" materials={["ACCESS FM template", "Your Week 1 user research", "Your specification page"]}>
1. **Write at least six spec points**, each **measurable and justified** and linked
   to a real need.
2. **Include** one **safety** point and one **sustainability** point.
3. **Peer check:** swap with a partner — can they trace each of your points back to a
   user need? Fix any that can't.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? A spec sentence frame">
Use this frame for every point:

> *"The product must **………** because **………** (measured by **………**)."*

Start from your user's own words — that guarantees each point is justified.
</Support>

<Section title="Homework & extension" icon="🏠" id="homework">

points, including safety and sustainability.

<Extension title="Go deeper">

things it does well and one weakness your design will fix.
</Extension>
