Now you find out — honestly — how well your product meets the brief. You **test it
objectively**, gather a little **user feedback**, and write an **evaluation** that
judges each spec point with evidence. Honesty scores; spin doesn't.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This is <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 7</ExamLink> (testing & evaluation, 10
  marks). The examiner rewards **measured evidence** and **justified improvements** —
  and, just like the written paper, generic words ("strong", "good") score nothing on
  their own.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Objective testing", definition: "Measuring a result, rather than giving an opinion." },
  { term: "Test against the spec", definition: "Checking the product point by point against your specification." },
  { term: "User feedback", definition: "What your real user says after trying the product." },
  { term: "Justified improvement", definition: "A specific change with a reason — not just 'make it better'." },
]} />

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Test, don't guess

Measure: how much **load** did it hold? Is it **rigid**? Does the **circuit** work
every time? Numbers and observations, not opinions.

### 2. Judge against the specification

Go through your **spec point by point**: state the result, say whether it was **met**,
and back it with evidence. Add brief **user feedback** — did it solve their problem?

### 3. Suggest justified improvements

For anything that fell short (or could be better), propose a **specific, justified**
improvement. "Add a wider base **because** it tipped at 5 kg" — not "make it stronger".

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — one spec point, evaluated">
1. **Spec point:** "must hold 5 kg".
2. **Test result:** loaded it to 6 kg before it started to flex.
3. **Judgement:** **met**, with a margin — evidence: it held 1 kg over the target.
4. **Improvement:** "a second brace would push the limit higher, *because* the flex started at the mid-rail."
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Test and evaluate" time="Full session" grouping="Individual (load rig shared)" materials={["Load rig & weights", "Test sheet", "User-feedback slip"]}>
1. **Run objective tests** (load, rigidity, circuit) and record the data.
2. **Gather brief user feedback** from the person you designed for, if you can.
3. **Write your evaluation** against each spec point, with **justified improvements**.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? An evaluation frame">
Use the writing frame for each point: **"Spec point → result → met? → improvement."**
A **ready-made results table** keeps your data tidy and easy to judge against.
</Support>
