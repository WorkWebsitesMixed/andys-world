<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Concrete — and why we reinforce it

Concrete is **strong in compression but weak in tension**, so on its own a concrete
beam cracks where it's stretched. We add **steel reinforcing bar** exactly where the
**tension** happens (the stretched face). Steel takes the tension, concrete takes the
compression — a perfect team.

### 2. Plastics and composites

- **Plastics:** *thermoplastics* (re-meltable, like acrylic) vs *thermosets* (set for
  good). Useful for light structural parts and housings.
- **Composites:** a **matrix + reinforcement** — like glass-reinforced plastic (GRP)
  or carbon fibre — giving a very high **strength-to-weight** ratio.

### 3. Designing sustainably — the 6Rs

**Rethink · Refuse · Reduce · Reuse · Repair · Recycle.** Apply them to every
material choice. Watch out: **electronics are hard to recycle** because they mix and
bond different materials — a reason to keep your circuit simple and removable.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — reinforcing a concrete beam">
1. **Load it:** a concrete beam carries weight in the middle, so it tends to bend.
2. **Find the tension:** bending stretches the **bottom** face — that's where it will crack.
3. **Add steel there:** place reinforcing bar **near the bottom**, in the tension zone.
4. **Result:** steel resists the tension, concrete resists the compression — the beam carries far more load.
</WorkedExample>

<Support title="Need a hand? A 6Rs prompt card">
Ask these about your product, in order:

- **Rethink** — does it even need this part?
- **Reduce** — can I use less material?
- **Reuse / Repair** — can a part be reused or easily fixed?
- **Recycle** — is the material recyclable, and can it be separated?

Pick the two that fit your product best and write them as spec points.
