<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Moments and equilibrium

A **moment** is a turning effect: **moment = force × distance** from the pivot. A
beam is in **equilibrium** when the turning effects balance:

> total clockwise moments = total anticlockwise moments

That single rule lets you find an unknown support **reaction**.

### 2. Stress

**Stress = force ÷ area** (units **N/m²**). For a round bar, the area is a circle:
**area = π r²**. Watch your units — convert millimetres to metres first.

### 3. Factor of Safety

Real designs are built **stronger than the expected load** — that margin is the
**Factor of Safety**. A hoist rated to lift 100 kg might be built to hold 500 kg: a
factor of safety of 5.

<Callout type="tip" title="The golden rules">
  Show every step · keep the units with the numbers · convert mm → m before using
  area · write the final answer with its unit.
</Callout>

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — stress, then a beam reaction">
**(a) Stress in a round pillar.** Force = 3140 N, radius = 10 mm = 0.01 m.
1. Area = π r² = π × (0.01)² = 3.14 × 10⁻⁴ m².
2. Stress = force ÷ area = 3140 ÷ (3.14 × 10⁻⁴).
3. Stress = **10 000 000 N/m² = 10 MPa.**

**(b) Beam reaction.** A 2 m beam rests on a support at each end. A 200 N load sits 1.5 m from the left support (R₁).
1. Take moments about R₁: clockwise = 200 × 1.5 = 300 N·m.
2. Anticlockwise = R₂ × 2.
3. Balance: R₂ × 2 = 300 → **R₂ = 150 N.**
4. Total up = total down: R₁ + R₂ = 200 → **R₁ = 50 N.**
</WorkedExample>

<Support title="Need a hand? The steps, pre-laid-out">
**Stress:** (1) change mm → m. (2) area = π r². (3) stress = force ÷ area. (4) write the unit (N/m²).

**Reaction:** (1) pick one support as the pivot. (2) clockwise moment = force × distance. (3) set it equal to (other reaction × beam length). (4) solve. (5) the two reactions add up to the total load.
