Before you make anything in Term 3, you need a **plan**: the order of operations, the
tools, the materials, and where things could go wrong. A good production plan is what
lets 60 students share a makerspace and all finish.

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. What goes in a production plan

- the **sequence of operations** (what you do, in order),
- the **tools and machines** for each step,
- a **cutting list** — materials, sizes and **quantities**,
- **time estimates**, and
- **quality-control** checkpoints and **safety** notes.

### 2. Plan for things going wrong

Spot your **critical steps** — the ones that hold everything up if they fail — and
write a **contingency** (a plan B) for each. Match your plan to the **shared
makerspace schedule** so the machines are free when you need them.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a six-step plan for the gear organiser">
1. **Cut** the sheet parts on the laser (QC: check sizes against the cutting list).
2. **Print** the brackets (QC: check they fit the slots).
3. **Dry-assemble** to check fit (QC checkpoint).
4. **Fix** the joints (safety: glue-gun care).
5. **Wire** the switch–LED circuit and test it.
6. **Finish** and final-check against the spec.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Write your production plan" time="50 min" grouping="Individual (technician advises)" materials={["Production-plan template", "Cutting-list template", "Machine-availability chart"]}>
