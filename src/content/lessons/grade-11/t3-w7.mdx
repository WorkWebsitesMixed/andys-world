'Snagging' is the building industry term for a final check of everything that is not quite right before a building is signed off. In product design it is the same idea: a systematic search for quality problems you can still fix before the product is photographed and evaluated. Today you apply the second finish coat and work through your snagging list.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  <ExamLink to="project">Component 2</ExamLink> criterion **C6 (making, 30 marks)** and criterion **C7 (evaluation, 15 marks)** both reward evidence that you evaluated your own making quality and addressed problems. A snagging list that identifies and fixes problems is C6 evidence. A snagging list that identifies problems and honestly discusses what could not be fixed is C7 evidence. Both earn marks.
</Callout>

<Section title="Snagging process" icon="🔍" id="snagging">

### How to run a snagging inspection

Work through the product systematically, category by category:

1. **Structure:** Any visible gaps at joints? Any panels out of square or not flush? Any splits or cracks?
2. **Surface:** Any scratches, dents, or uneven finish from coat 1? Any areas of raised grain?
3. **Electronics:** All components securely mounted? Wiring neat and routed without crossing external surfaces? Switch operates cleanly?
4. **Function:** Does the LED light when switched on? Does it extinguish when off? Do all pegs sit vertically?
5. **Dimensions:** Is the product within the specified footprint (400 × 400 mm maximum)? Is the overall height as designed?

For each issue found, note:
- **What:** description of the problem
- **Severity:** minor (cosmetic only) / moderate (affects appearance from 1 m away) / major (affects function or structural integrity)
- **Fix:** what you will do, or why it cannot be fixed at this stage

</Section>

<Activity title="Snagging + second finish coat" time="90 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["Product from W6 (first coat cured)", "320 grit sandpaper or grey Scotch-Brite pad", "Tack cloth", "Hardwax oil or finish", "Lint-free cloth", "Snagging list template", "Filler (sanding dust + PVA, or wood filler)", "Camera"]}>

**Snagging inspection (0–20 min):**
1. Work through the five categories (structure, surface, electronics, function, dimensions).
2. For each issue: record what, severity, and fix.

**Fix snaggable items (20–50 min):**
3. Address all minor and moderate issues that can be fixed: fill gaps, re-seat loose components, clean wiring.
4. Photograph before and after any fix.

**De-nib and second coat (50–80 min):**
5. Lightly sand all external faces with 320 grit or grey Scotch-Brite pad. Remove all raised grain.
6. Remove dust with tack cloth.
7. Apply finish coat 2. Work it in, leave to penetrate, buff.

**Making log (80–90 min):**
8. Complete the T3 W7 making log entry: snagging list, fixes applied, second coat status.
</Activity>

<Support title="Can't fix a snagging item?">

**Joint gap too large to fill with sanding dust?** Use a flexible wood filler (coloured to match the wood). Apply with a palette knife, slightly proud. Allow to cure, sand flush. The repair should be invisible from 300 mm.

**Finish running or dripping?** Too much finish applied in one coat. Let it cure fully (24 h), then de-nib the run with 320 grit and apply the third coat very thinly. The drip will be absorbed into the surface.

**Issue that genuinely cannot be fixed?** Document it honestly in the snagging list with an explanation of why it cannot be fixed at this stage. An honest C7 evaluation identifies this issue and explains what you would do differently in a production version of the product. This earns C7 marks.
</Support>
