Your product is finished — now you **prove it works** and **capture it well**. Because
the made product isn't sent to the examiner, your **photographs are the evidence** of
all your making. This is the week your realisation is complete.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This completes <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 6</ExamLink> (realisation, **30 marks
  — the biggest single criterion**). The moderator marks from your **photos and folder**,
  not the object, so the photographs really matter.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. What "realised" means

A **realised** product is complete, **functions as intended**, and is made to a good
standard. Confirm both required features: it **carries the stated load**, and the
**switch-operated light works**.

### 2. Photograph for evidence

The made product is **not submitted** — your **photos are**. So shoot:

- an **overall view** (clean background),
- the product **in use under load**,
- a **detail of the working light** (switch on),
- a **join or finish detail** to show making quality.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a strong evidence photo set">
1. **Overall view** on a plain backdrop — the whole product, well lit.
2. **In use:** the load on it (state the weight), nothing sagging.
3. **Light on:** a close shot of the indicator working.
4. **Detail:** a tidy joint and finish, to evidence craftsmanship.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Finish, prove, photograph" time="Full session" grouping="Photo station rotation" materials={["Your product", "Load weights", "Photo station / backdrop"]}>
1. **Finalise** any last touches on your product.
2. **Confirm the required features work** — load held *and* light on.
3. **Photograph it** for your folder, following the shot list above.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? A photo checklist">
Use the **photo checklist** (overall ✓, in-use under load ✓, light detail ✓, join
detail ✓) and ask for help **staging the shots** — good light and a plain background
make a big difference.
</Support>
