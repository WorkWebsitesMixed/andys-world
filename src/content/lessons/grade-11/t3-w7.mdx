M6 has been marked. Every student now has specific evidence of where their marks are going. This session is individually differentiated: each student works from their own M6 gap analysis, targeting their two lowest-scoring areas with a focused question set.

<Callout type="info" title="Differentiated session format" id="diff">
  This session is not a whole-class session. The teacher will brief each student individually on their M6 analysis and direct them to the appropriate question bank section. Students who scored highly across all areas work on Section B extended answer quality and evaluation language.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Targeted practice", definition: "Revising specifically the topic and question type where marks were lost — not the topic that feels comfortable. Evidence-based: your M6 paper tells you exactly where to spend the next 90 minutes." },
  { term: "Deliberate practice", definition: "Practice that focuses on weaknesses rather than strengths, operates at the edge of competence (not so easy it requires no effort, not so hard it produces no learning), and includes immediate feedback. The question banks and self-marking sheets for each topic are designed to provide immediate feedback." },
  { term: "Retrieval fluency", definition: "The speed and ease with which information can be recalled under exam conditions. Retrieval fluency improves with repeated active recall practice — the same material covered multiple times with increasing time gaps. If structures calculations feel slow, it means more practice repetitions are needed." },
]} />

<Section title="Topic question banks — which to use" icon="📋" id="banks">

### Route A: Structures priority

Use the structures question bank. Start with: identification questions (force types, structure types), then FoS calculations, then stress calculations, then beam reactions. Target: 80% on 10 questions before moving to Section B extended structures questions.

### Route B: Materials priority

Use the materials question bank. Start with: material family identification (softwoods/hardwoods/composites), then property values recall, then application questions (justify a material choice), then vocabulary rewrite exercises. Target: full marks on 6 vocabulary rewrites before moving to comparison questions.

### Route C: Electronics priority

Use the electronics question bank. Start with: unit conversion drill (mA to A, kΩ to Ω), then IPO labelling, then resistor calculations, then voltage divider calculations. Target: 80% on 10 calculations with all working shown.

### Route D: Design process and Paper 1 priority

Use the Paper 1 practice set. Focus on: part (a) function points (3 attempts at different contexts), part (c) timed idea annotation (3 ideas in 15 minutes), part (g) evaluation language (3 evaluation entries with evidence and modification suggestions).

### Route E: Section B extended answers priority

Use the Section B extended answer bank. For each 6-mark question: write the 6-point margin plan before writing the answer. After writing: compare to mark scheme and count distinct mark points earned. Target: 6 distinct mark points per 6-mark answer, 4 per 4-mark answer.

</Section>

<Section title="The self-correction loop" icon="🔄" id="loop">

For every incorrectly answered question in today's session:

1. **Read the mark scheme answer** for that question.
2. **Identify the gap:** Was it knowledge (I did not know this)? Vocabulary (I knew it but used generic words)? Method (I knew the formula but made a calculation error)?
3. **Write the correct answer** in full, from memory, without looking at the mark scheme.
4. **Move to the next question** — do not re-attempt the same question again immediately. Return to it in 20 minutes (the minimum gap for retrieval benefit).

</Section>

<Activity title="Personalised targeted revision" time="90 min" grouping="Individual (differentiated)" materials={["Your M6 marked paper", "Appropriate question bank (route A–E as directed by teacher)", "Mark scheme for question bank", "Calculator (route C)"]}>

**Setup (0–10 min):**
1. Write your two lowest-scoring topics from M6 and your percentage in each.
2. Select your route (A–E or a combination as directed).
3. Set a target: "I will achieve at least 80% on [topic] by the end of this session."

**First priority topic (10–55 min):**
4. Complete the question bank for your first priority. Self-mark after each question. Apply the self-correction loop for every wrong answer.
5. Track your running total as you go. When you hit 80% on a set of 10, note the time.

**Second priority topic (55–85 min):**
6. Switch to your second priority topic. Same approach.

**Review and plan (85–90 min):**
7. Write your final scores for both topics. Compare to M6 baseline.
8. Write one sentence: what do you still need to do before the exam for each topic? What form should that practice take (more questions? Vocabulary cards? Formula drilling?)?
</Activity>

<Support title="Each route — the single most common error">

**Route A (Structures):** Beam reaction formula — always double-check which distance goes in the numerator. RA = W x (distance from load to B) / span. The reaction at A equals the proportion of W that is "closer to B."

**Route B (Materials):** The vocabulary trap — "MDF has no grain" earns 1 mark. "MDF has no grain direction, which means it can be laser-cut in any orientation without splitting along a grain line" earns 2 marks. Always add the consequence.

**Route C (Electronics):** Unit conversion first. Before writing any formula: I_LED = [value] mA = [value/1000] A. Write this conversion on the paper as the first line of working, even before writing the formula.

**Route D (Design process):** Function points that describe appearance ("it must be blue") or material ("it must be made of MDF") rather than function ("it must store five items") earn zero. Every function point must describe what the product DOES.

**Route E (Section B):** Context specificity — every sentence must connect to the specific design context in the question. "Mild steel is strong" earns 0. "Mild steel has a tensile strength of approximately 400 MPa, sufficient to support the stated 25 kg load on the kitchen rack without deformation" earns the mark.
</Support>
