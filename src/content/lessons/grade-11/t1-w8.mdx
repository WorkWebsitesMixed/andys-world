A structure is only as good as its **joints**. This week you learn how members are
held together, how to stop a joint from working loose, and how to **reinforce** weak
points — all of which you'll use when you make your product.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  Joining methods are a regular <ExamLink to="paper4">**Paper 4 Section B**</ExamLink> question — often "sketch a joint
  (with a means of preventing movement)" or "give a reason for choosing a bolt / rivet
  / weld". It also feeds your project's planning and making.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Temporary joint", definition: "A joint that can be undone — a nut & bolt, or screws." },
  { term: "Permanent joint", definition: "A joint that can't easily be undone — a rivet, weld or adhesive." },
  { term: "Reinforcement", definition: "Adding gussets, ribs, braces or laminating to strengthen a joint or member." },
  { term: "KD fitting", definition: "A knock-down fitting that joins flat boards and can be dismantled (flat-pack furniture)." },
  { term: "Anti-movement feature", definition: "Something that locks a joint so it can't shift — a dowel, a second fixing, or adhesive." },
]} />

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Temporary vs permanent

- **Temporary** joints can be undone: **nut & bolt**, **screws**. Good for repair,
  flat-pack and access.
- **Permanent** joints can't: **rivet**, **weld**, **adhesive**. Strong and tidy, but
  there's no going back.

### 2. Stop it moving

A single fixing can still rotate or slide. Add an **anti-movement feature** — a
**dowel**, a **second fixing**, or **adhesive** — so the joint stays put under load.
In the exam, always draw this detail.

### 3. Reinforcement

Strengthen weak corners and members with **gussets**, **ribs**, **braces** or
**laminating**. For board products, **KD fittings** join panels and still allow
flat-pack assembly.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — bolt vs rivet vs weld">
1. **Bolt** — *"Choose a bolt where the joint may need to be **undone** later (for repair or adjustment)."*
2. **Rivet** — *"Choose a rivet for a **permanent**, light joint in sheet metal that won't be taken apart."*
3. **Weld** — *"Choose a weld for the **strongest permanent** joint between two steel members."*
4. **Anti-movement** — at a timber truss node, add a **dowel** through the joint so the members can't rotate.
</WorkedExample>
