<Section title="Part 1 (0–45 min): Idea generation for C3" icon="💡" id="ideas">

### The fixation trap — and how to escape it

Most students generate idea 1 in 10 minutes and then spend 20 minutes producing two near-identical variations of it. The result: three sketches that are essentially the same product from three angles.

To avoid this, each idea must start from a different structural principle:

| Structural principle | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Cantilever | Load extends beyond a single support point | Wall-mounted shelf with one fixed arm, projecting load |
| Frame and panel | Outer frame with infill panels | A freestanding desk organiser with a timber frame and MDF panel sides |
| Tension system | Load carried by tension members (cables, cords) | Floating shelves supported by steel cables anchored above |
| Folded plate | A single sheet of material bent into structural form | A single sheet of acrylic bent into an L-shelf bracket |
| Mass | Solid load-bearing body | A thick-walled plinth or base that carries load by mass |

Choose three different principles. Sketch one for each.

### SCAMPER in practice

If you finish idea 1 and idea 2 is not forming, apply SCAMPER to idea 1:

- **Substitute:** What if the vertical support were a cable instead of a timber strut?
- **Combine:** What if the shelf and the circuit mounting were the same component?
- **Eliminate:** What if you removed the back panel — does the structure still work?
- **Reverse:** What if the load were suspended from below rather than resting on top?

One SCAMPER question usually produces an idea that looks nothing like idea 1.

### Annotation against the spec (not just labels)

For each idea, your annotations must reference the specification:
- "Horizontal arm — carries 5 kg load (Spec point 1), 200 mm deep (Spec point 3)."
- "KD fittings at joints — allows disassembly for recycling (Spec point 6 — sustainability)."
- "Toggle switch on right-hand face — accessible to right-handed user (Spec point 5)."

This links C3 to C2 — a key requirement for marks.

<Section title="Paper 1 structure — seven parts" icon="📄" id="paper1">

### Parts (a) to (g) at a glance

| Part | What is asked | Key technique |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | Function points for the given brief | Three distinct functions, each beginning "The product must..." |
| (b) | Target user description | Specific person (age, context, needs), not generic |
| (c) | Three annotated design ideas | Three structurally different ideas; material, join, function annotated on each |
| (d) | Development of one idea | Show a design change + explain why + how it improves the product |
| (e) | Sustainability | Apply one or two of the 6Rs to the design |
| (f) | Testing criteria | Two testable criteria from the specification; state how each would be tested |
| (g) | Evaluation statement | One sentence per spec point: met or not met, with evidence |

### Time management for M3

- Part (a): 5 minutes (function points — practised in T2 W1)
- Part (b): 3 minutes (user description)
- Part (c): 15 minutes (three ideas — 5 minutes per idea)
- Parts (d)–(g): 22 minutes total (roughly 5–6 minutes each)

Total: 45 minutes. Move on after the allocated time whether or not you feel ready.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Three structurally different ideas for a musician's organiser">

**Idea 1 — Cantilever shelf:** A single horizontal MDF shelf projecting from the wall, supported by a diagonal steel bracket below (compression strut). No visible support above. The structural principle is cantilever — the fixing point at the wall takes both downward force and the bending moment.

Annotation: "Steel diagonal brace — compression member resists the downward bending moment. MDF shelf — 18 mm thick, calculated to resist 5 kg midspan load. Toggle switch at right-hand end — accessible without removing items."

**Idea 2 — Folded acrylic:** A single sheet of 5 mm acrylic bent at 90° using a line bender — the horizontal section becomes the shelf, the vertical section becomes the back panel and provides the fixing point. Structural principle: folded plate (moment of inertia increased by the 90° fold).

**Idea 3 — Tension cable:** Two vertical steel cables fixed to the ceiling above and a wall hook below. Two glass shelves clip onto cable brackets. Structural principle: tension system — the cables are in tension, the shelves rest in compression on the clips.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Idea generation and Paper 1 mock" time="90 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["A3 paper for ideas (Part 1)", "Exam paper for M3 (Part 2)", "Your design brief (on desk throughout)", "Black pen for exam, pencil for sketches"]}>

**Part 1 — Idea generation (0–45 min):**
1. Choose three structural principles from the list — one for each idea. Write the principle name at the top of each idea box before drawing anything.
2. Sketch idea 1: isometric or oblique view, thick/thin line, four annotations (material, join, function, dimension), each annotation linked to a specific specification point.
3. Sketch idea 2: different structural principle, same annotation standard.
4. Sketch idea 3: different structural principle again. If stuck, apply SCAMPER to idea 1.
5. Review: are all three ideas genuinely different? If two look like the same form, start the weaker one again from scratch.

**Part 2 — M3 Paper 1 mock (45–90 min):**
6. Sit in exam conditions. Parts (a)–(g) of Paper 1 applied to your own project brief.
7. Your design brief stays on your desk — you are answering questions about your own project.
8. Move on after the allocated time per part. Do not spend all 45 minutes on part (c).
</Activity>

<Support title="Idea generation scaffolds">

**Starting idea 2 when blank:** Open your specification. Find the spec point about the circuit. Now design the product around the circuit — start from where the switch must go, and build the structure outward from there. This forces a structurally different approach to the one you used for idea 1.

**Annotation feels thin?** For each annotation, add a second sentence that links to the spec. "18 mm MDF — laser cuts cleanly (workshop constraint) and meets the load requirement at this thickness (Spec point 1)." Two-sentence annotations almost always earn the mark; one-sentence annotations sometimes miss.

**Part (c) of Paper 1 running over time?** Three ideas in 15 minutes means 5 minutes per idea. After 5 minutes, the idea is done whether or not you are satisfied. A rushed idea 3 earns partial marks; a missing idea 3 earns zero.
</Support>

<Section title="Homework and term review" icon="🏠" id="homework">

**Home task:** Review your three C3 ideas. Select the strongest for development in T2. Write a 60-word justification for your selection: which specification points does it best meet, and why is the structural principle the most appropriate for your user?
