You have a user, a brief and a specification. Now the fun part: **generating ideas**.
This is the single biggest chunk of coursework marks, and it rewards having lots of
**genuinely different** ideas — not one idea drawn five times.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This launches <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 3</ExamLink> (idea generation) — worth
  **20 marks**, the largest single criterion. The same skill is <ExamLink to="paper1">**Paper 1 part (c)**</ExamLink>
  (12 marks for three developed ideas). Annotating ideas *against your spec* is where
  the marks are.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Beat design fixation

Your first idea is rarely your best — it's just the most obvious. To score well you
need ideas that are **conceptually different**: a cyclist's gear organiser as a
*wall-mounted pegboard*, as a *freestanding tower*, and as a *low hanging frame* are
three concepts; the same organiser in three colours is one.

### 2. Strategies to spark ideas

- **SCAMPER** — push your idea through each prompt (Substitute a material? Combine two
  functions? Reverse the layout?).
- **Morphological analysis** — list options for each part and mix them.
- **Fast thumbnails** — many small quick sketches, no rubbing out.

### 3. Annotate against the spec

This is the marks step: on each idea, add notes linking it to your **specification**
points — "holds the gear weight via a triangulated frame", "switch-operated light
meets the 'ready to go' requirement".

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — three concepts for a cyclist's gear organiser">
1. **Concept A — wall-mounted pegboard frame:** hooks and a shelf on a braced board. *Off the floor, few parts; meets the 'small footprint' spec.*
2. **Concept B — freestanding tower frame:** a triangulated upright that stands by the door. *Strong and movable; meets the 'holds gear weight' spec.*
3. **Concept C — low hanging frame:** a suspended rail the gear hangs from. *Quick to grab; meets the 'fast access' spec.*

→ Three **conceptually different** structures, each annotated to a spec point.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Generate three ideas" time="55 min" grouping="Individual (teacher circulates)" materials={["Your specification", "Idea-per-box template", "SCAMPER prompt cards"]}>
1. **Sketch at least three conceptually different ideas** for your product.
2. **Annotate each** with materials, construction, and the **spec points** it meets.
3. **Take one creative risk** — try an unusual idea and note *why* you tried it, even
   if it doesn't make the final cut.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? SCAMPER prompts + an idea-per-box grid">
Use the **idea-per-box** grid so each box only needs one quick idea — no blank-page
pressure. If you're stuck, run your idea through **SCAMPER**:

- **S**ubstitute a material? **C**ombine two parts? **A**dapt something that exists?
- **M**odify the size/shape? **P**ut it to another use? **E**liminate a part?
  **R**everse the layout?
</Support>
