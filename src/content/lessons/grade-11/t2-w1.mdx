Welcome to Term 2. You ended last term with several different ideas; now you
**develop** them, choose **one** to build, and — crucially — explain **why**. Making
that choice *objectively*, against your specification, is what turns a nice sketch
into a high-scoring design decision.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This **closes <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 3</ExamLink>** (idea generation,
  20 marks) and rehearses <ExamLink to="paper1">**Paper 1 part (d)**</ExamLink> — evaluate three ideas, choose one, and
  justify it. The justification is the marks; "I just liked it" scores nothing.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Develop, don't just generate

Generating ideas (Term 1) was about *breadth*. **Developing** is about *depth*: add
dimensions, show how parts join, resolve the structure and the circuit. A developed
idea answers "but how would that actually work?"

### 2. Choose objectively — the evaluation matrix

Don't pick the one you *like*. Score each idea against your **specification** in a
matrix. Give your most important spec points a **weighting** so they count for more.
The highest total points you to the strongest design — fairly.

### 3. Justify your choice

State **why** the winner best meets the spec. This is the exact skill of **Paper 1
part (d)**: "Idea B best meets the spec because it supports the load *and* fits the
small desk, which the user said matters most."

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — an evaluation matrix for a cyclist's gear organiser">
1. **List your key spec points** and weight them (×2 for the most important).
2. **Score each idea** out of 5 on each point.
3. **Multiply by the weight, then total** each column.
4. **Read off the winner** and justify it against the user's biggest need.

| Spec point (weight) | Wall pegboard | Freestanding tower | Hanging frame |
|---|---|---|---|
| Holds gear weight (×2) | 4 → 8 | 5 → 10 | 3 → 6 |
| Fast to grab (×2) | 5 → 10 | 3 → 6 | 5 → 10 |
| Easy to make (×1) | 4 | 3 | 3 |
| **Total** | **22** | **19** | **19** |

→ *"The wall pegboard scores highest because it best balances the user's two
priorities — holding the gear **and** being quick to grab before a ride."*
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Develop, evaluate, decide" time="50 min" grouping="Individual (teacher + drawing colleague circulate)" materials={["Your Term 1 idea pages", "Weighted evaluation-matrix template", "Your specification"]}>
1. **Develop** each of your three ideas — add detail so each clearly *works*.
2. **Build an evaluation matrix** scoring them against your weighted spec points.
3. **Select one** and write a short **justification** of why it wins.

This completes **C3** — developed ideas + matrix + justified chosen design.
</Activity>

<Section title="Homework & extension" icon="🏠" id="homework">

**Homework:** tidy your **chosen-design page**, and list everything you'll need to
**test** during development (does it hold the load? is it rigid? does the circuit
work?).

<Extension title="Go deeper">

and score it on your matrix — does it beat the original winner?
