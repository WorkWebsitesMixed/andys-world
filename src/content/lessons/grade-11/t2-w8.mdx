A **working drawing** is the instruction manual for your product: clear enough that
someone *else* could build it. This week you produce a dimensioned, conventionally
laid-out drawing to British Standards.

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Three views, by convention

A **third-angle orthographic** drawing lays out the **plan, front elevation and end
elevation** in standard positions, so anyone reading it knows what they're looking at.

<WatchSee items={[
  { label: "Third-angle orthographic projection (beginners)", href: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qzE9ZZYCfeY", kind: "Video", note: "How the plan, front and end views are arranged." }
]} />

### 2. Scale and dimensioning

State a **scale** (e.g. 1:2) and dimension to **British Standards**: clear
extension and dimension lines, the **diameter symbol (⌀)** for holes, centre lines,
and **hidden detail** as dashed lines.

### 3. Make it buildable

Add a **title block** and an **assembly note**, and a **section or exploded view**
where it helps. The test: could someone build it from this drawing alone?

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — dimensioning a bracket">
1. **Lay out** the plan, front and end elevations in third-angle positions.
2. **State the scale** (say 1:2) in the title block.
3. **Dimension** each view to British Standards — sizes, hole diameters (⌀), centre lines.
4. **Show hidden detail** (a hole behind a face) as dashed lines.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Draw it to standard" time="55 min" grouping="Individual (drawing colleague circulates)" materials={["CAD or drawing board", "BS dimensioning guide", "Your 3D model & parts"]}>

2. **Dimension** them to British Standards, with a stated scale.
