This week you sit a **full, timed Paper 1** from start to finish. It's a rehearsal
under real conditions — the best way to build exam stamina and timing. This page is
about **how to prepare and approach it** so you finish strong.

  Everything you've practised — sketching, three different ideas, justifying,

<Section title="How to approach the paper" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Read the context first

You choose **one** of three everyday-product contexts. Read all three, pick the one
you can picture most clearly, and stick with it.

### 2. Spend time where the marks are

Parts **(c) and (e) are worth 12 marks each** — almost half the paper. Don't burn
your time perfecting part (a). A rough mark-led plan for 75 minutes:

- (a) 4 marks → ~5 min
- (b) 4 marks → ~6 min
- **(c) 12 marks → ~18 min**
- (d) 8 marks → ~10 min
- **(e) 12 marks → ~18 min**
- (f) 4 marks → ~5 min
- (g) 6 marks → ~8 min
- review → ~5 min

### 3. If you get stuck, move on

A blank part scores nothing, but so does an unfinished part you're still polishing.
Leave it, do the next, come back. Keep **sketching and annotating** throughout.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a quick time plan in your head">
1. **Glance at the clock** and note your finish time.
2. **Mark the half-way point:** you should be starting part (e) at roughly 40 minutes in.
3. **Protect (c) and (e):** if you're behind, trim parts (a), (b) and (f), never the 12-markers.
4. **Save 5 minutes** at the end to add missing labels and dimensions.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Sit the mock" time="75 min timed + reflection" grouping="Individual, exam conditions" materials={["The mock paper", "Sketching equipment", "A watch or visible clock"]}>
Complete the **full timed Paper 1** on a fresh context, parts (a)–(g). Manage your
time with the plan above. When you finish, note **where your focus dipped** and one
thing you'll do differently next time.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? Access & a timing card">
If you have **access arrangements** (extra time, a reduced context), use them. Keep
a small **timing card** with the minutes-per-part plan beside you so you don't have
to hold it in your head.
</Support>
