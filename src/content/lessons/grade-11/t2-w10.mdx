<Section title="Paper 4 preparation reminders" icon="📋" id="prep">

### The five most-tested topics

Based on past papers from 2019–2024:

1. **Electronics:** Ohm's Law calculation, LED circuit, transistor switch (which component is missing / what is its function)
2. **Structural forces:** beam reactions, triangulation in frames, forces in tension and compression
3. **Materials:** properties table (give one advantage of X material for this use), timber types, finishing
4. **Mechanisms:** gear ratio calculation, cam types, linkage function
5. **Sustainability:** one-mark question asking for a sustainability advantage of a named material or process decision

### Exam technique checklist

- **Read the question carefully:** identify the command word before writing anything.
- **1 mark = 1 clear point:** don't pad answers — one mark does not need three sentences.
- **Calculations:** formula then substitute then answer with units. Do not skip steps.
- **Diagrams:** label them — unlabelled diagrams score zero even if drawn correctly.
- **If stuck:** answer every part with something, then return. A blank earns zero; an attempt may earn 1 mark.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked electronics question — LED circuit">

**Question:** A circuit contains a 9 V battery, a switch, a resistor R, and a red LED. The LED has a forward voltage of 2 V and requires a current of 20 mA. Calculate the value of R. Show your working. (3 marks)

**Step 1 — Formula:** V = IR, rearranged to R = V ÷ I

**Step 2 — Voltage across R:** V_R = V_supply − V_LED = 9 − 2 = 7 V

**Step 3 — Calculate R:** R = 7 ÷ 0.02 = **350 Ω**

In practice the nearest standard value is 360 Ω — note this if the question asks for a practical value.

**Mark allocation:** 1 mark for correct formula/approach; 1 mark for correct voltage subtraction; 1 mark for correct answer with units.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Graded M3 — Paper 4 past paper" time="90 min" grouping="Individual — exam conditions" materials={["Paper 4 past paper script (teacher issued)", "Calculator", "Ruler, pencil, pen", "No notes"]}>

**Exam (0–75 min):**
1. Exam conditions. No notes, no phones. Calculator permitted.
2. Write your name on every page of the script.
3. Answer every question. If stuck, move on and return — do not leave blanks.
4. Check your answers in the final 10 minutes: units on calculations, labels on diagrams.

**Post-exam review (75–90 min):**
5. Scripts collected at 75 minutes.
6. Teacher issues the mark scheme for the first two questions only. Quick self-check: were your command-word interpretations correct? Did you write state answers for state questions?
7. Write on a sticky note: "My Paper 4 weakest topic is ____. I will address this by ____." Put it in your folder.
</Activity>

<Support title="Paper 4 feels overwhelming?">

**Too many topics to revise?** You do not need to master everything equally. Use your baseline score to identify your weakest two topics. Revise those two first — they have the highest return on revision time.

**Calculation questions always lose marks?** The three steps — formula, substitution, answer with units — are a habit, not knowledge. Practise until it is automatic: write the formula before you do any arithmetic, every time.

**Diagrams losing marks?** Every diagram needs a title and labels on every feature mentioned in the question. Draw a box around your diagram to distinguish it from the surrounding text.
</Support>
