<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. 3D CAD basics

Most parts follow the same path: **sketch a profile → extrude it into a solid →
modify** (holes, fillets, cuts). Build your part from simple shapes.

### 2. When to 3D print vs laser-cut

- **3D print** small or complex parts: brackets, connectors, feet, housings.
- **Laser-cut** flat parts from sheet: panels, plates, frames.

### 3. Print considerations

Think about **orientation** (affects strength and surface), **supports** (needed
for overhangs), and **time/material** (smaller and flatter prints faster).

### 4. Begin your working drawing

Start the **orthographic views** of your whole product — plan, front and end — which
you'll dimension next week.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a corner bracket, print-ready">
1. **Sketch** the bracket's L-shaped profile.
2. **Extrude** it to the right thickness.
3. **Modify:** add fixing holes and a fillet for strength.
4. **Orient** it flat on the bed so it needs **minimal supports** and prints quickly.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Model and set up a print" time="50 min" grouping="Workstations (technician supports)" materials={["CAD workstation", "3D printer access", "3D CAD quick-reference"]}>

2. **Set up the print** — choose orientation and supports.

</Activity>
