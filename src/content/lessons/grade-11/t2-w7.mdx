The product is made. The specification was written in T1 W8. This session completes the Component 2 cycle: you test the product against each specification point, record evidence, and write an evaluation that earns marks through honesty and specificity — not through claiming everything is perfect.

<Callout type="exam" title="Graded M4 (50%) — C7 evaluation collected at end of session" id="m4">
  **Moment 4 (M4, 50%)** is the largest graded moment of the year. Your C7 testing records and evaluation are collected at the end of this session. Each specification point must appear in the evaluation with: (a) the test method used, (b) the test result, and (c) a met/partially met/not met judgement with specific evidence. No generic statements will earn marks.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Testing protocol", definition: "A predefined procedure for testing a specification point. It specifies: what is measured, how it is measured, what equipment is used, how many measurements are taken, and what constitutes a pass result. A testing protocol ensures the test can be replicated and the result is objective." },
  { term: "Evaluation", definition: "A critical judgement of how well the product meets the specification, based on testing evidence. An effective evaluation: uses specific test results (not impressions), gives a judgement for each specification point, identifies the cause of any shortfall, and proposes an improvement. An evaluation is not a list of compliments." },
  { term: "Modification suggestion", definition: "For each specification point that was not fully met, a specific improvement that would change the result. A modification suggestion must be: specific (not 'make it stronger'), feasible (achievable with available processes), and linked to the shortfall (the cause of the 'not met' judgement)." },
  { term: "User feedback", definition: "Evidence from the target user about whether the product meets their needs. Collected after the user has used or handled the product. User feedback enriches the evaluation — it provides evidence that the designer alone cannot provide (subjective response to aesthetics, comfort of operation, fit into their space)." },
]} />

<Section title="The testing protocol" icon="🧪" id="testing">

### Testing each specification point

For each specification point, follow this protocol:
1. **State the test method:** How will you test this point? What equipment? How many trials?
2. **Conduct the test:** Record raw data (numbers, pass/fail, measurements).
3. **Compare to specification:** Does the result meet the criterion and tolerance?
4. **Judge:** Met / Partially met / Not met.

### Testing the six spec points from T1 W8

| Spec point | Test method |
|---|---|
| Load (5 kg static, less than 2 mm deflection) | Apply calibrated 5 kg deadweight for 5 minutes. Measure midspan deflection with a ruler and feeler gauge. |
| Dimensions (width, depth, height within tolerance) | Measure each overall dimension with a steel rule. Compare to specification value ±1 mm. |
| Aesthetics (no visible machining marks at 300 mm) | Visual inspection from 300 mm under standard lighting. Record any visible defects. |
| Material/sustainability (one 6Rs feature stated and visible) | Visual check that the stated feature (e.g. disassembly with screws) is present and functional. |
| Safety (all edges chamfered or rounded, no sharp projections) | Run a bare hand along all edges and corners. Record any sharp areas. |
| Circuit (LED lights when switch closed; extinguishes when switch open) | Insert battery. Toggle switch on: record if LED lights. Toggle off: record if LED extinguishes. Repeat 5 times. |

</Section>

<Section title="Writing the evaluation" icon="✏️" id="evaluation">

### The three-part evaluation entry

For each specification point, write:
1. **Test evidence:** what the test found (specific numbers, observations).
2. **Judgement:** met / partially met / not met.
3. **Modification suggestion** (if not fully met): what specific change would improve the result.

### Honesty earns marks

Mark schemes for evaluations explicitly credit honest, specific assessment. A statement like "the product easily met the load requirement — no deflection was observed" earns zero marks if no measurement was taken. A statement like "the deflection test recorded 3.2 mm at 5 kg. The specification required less than 2 mm. This specification point was not met. To achieve the required stiffness, the shelf would need to be increased to 24 mm MDF (calculated from the formula in T1 W3) or the shelf span reduced from 200 mm to 160 mm" earns full marks.

### Suggested modifications

A modification suggestion must:
- Name the specific change (not just "make it better").
- Link it to the cause of the shortfall.
- Confirm that the modification is feasible (the process and material exist).

Example: "The aesthetic test showed brush marks visible at 300 mm under side lighting. To meet the specification, the surface should be finished using a spray paint rather than a brush — this would produce a smooth, even coat with no visible application marks."

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Evaluation entry for two specification points">

**Spec point 1 — Load (5 kg, less than 2 mm deflection):**
Test: a 5 kg calibrated weight was placed at the shelf midspan for 5 minutes. Deflection measured using a steel rule held against the shelf underside before and after loading. Before: 0 mm. After 5 minutes: 1.6 mm. After removing weight: 0 mm (elastic recovery — permanent set: 0 mm).
Judgement: MET. The deflection of 1.6 mm is within the specification requirement of less than 2 mm. The elastic recovery to 0 mm confirms the deformation was elastic and no permanent damage occurred.

**Spec point 3 — Dimensions (width must not exceed 550 mm):**
Test: width measured with a steel rule at three positions along the shelf. Results: 547 mm, 548 mm, 548 mm. Mean: 547.7 mm.
Judgement: MET. The mean width of 547.7 mm is within the specification limit of 550 mm, with a margin of 2.3 mm. All three measurements were within the ±1 mm manufacturing tolerance stated in C5.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Testing and evaluation writing" time="90 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["Finished product", "5 kg deadweight (bag of sand or calibrated weight)", "Steel rule (600 mm)", "Feeler gauge (if available)", "Battery (9 V)", "Pencil and C7 pro forma"]}>

**Testing (0–40 min):**
1. Set up the product on a flat surface.
2. Work through the six spec points in order. For each: conduct the test, record raw data, record a judgement. Do not skip any test — a missing test = zero marks for that evaluation entry.

**User test (30–40 min, overlapping with testing):**
3. Ask a classmate (acting as your user) to operate the switch, place items on the shelf, and assess the finish from 300 mm. Record their comments verbatim.

**Evaluation writing (40–85 min):**
4. Write a formal evaluation entry for each specification point. Use the three-part format: test evidence + judgement + modification suggestion (if not fully met).
5. For any "not met" or "partially met" point: write a specific modification suggestion that would improve the result. Explain how the modification addresses the cause of the shortfall.

**Folder final check (85–90 min):**
6. Count: six spec points tested, six evaluation entries written. All entries have test evidence (numbers or clear observations)? All entries have a judgement? All "not met" entries have a modification suggestion? Submit C7 folder section.
</Activity>

<Support title="Evaluation writing help">

**"My product met all the specification points."** Are you sure — or are you just saying it met them? For each point: show the number. If no test was conducted, the mark scheme cannot credit the evaluation. An evaluation without numbers is an opinion, not an assessment.

**"I do not know how to write the modification suggestion."** The modification must fix the specific cause of the shortfall. If the aesthetic point was not met because of brush marks: the cause is brush application. The modification is to use a spray finish. If the load point was not met because the shelf deflected 3.5 mm: the cause is insufficient stiffness. The modification is to increase shelf thickness or reduce span.

**"User feedback says the product is ugly."** This is valuable evidence, not a problem. Write: "User feedback: '[quote verbatim]'. The aesthetic specification point was therefore partially met by the user's assessment. To improve: [specific modification]."
</Support>
