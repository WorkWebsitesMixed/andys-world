You cannot use the makerspace until you complete the induction. This session covers every machine, every hazard, and every emergency procedure you will encounter during Term 3 making. The induction quiz at the end must be passed at 100% — there is no partial credit for workshop safety.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  Safe working is assessed in <ExamLink to="project">Component 2</ExamLink> criterion **C6 (making)**. The mark scheme includes marks for evidence of safe working practices — correct PPE, correct tool selection, and safe machine operation visible in the making log photographs. Beyond the marks: an injury in the makerspace is a real event with real consequences.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)", definition: "Equipment worn to reduce the risk of injury: safety glasses (eye protection from chips and laser flash), apron (protection from hot debris), ear protection (for sustained machine noise above 85 dB), dust mask (for sanding or machining wood composites). PPE must be worn — not kept in a pocket." },
  { term: "COSHH", definition: "Control of Substances Hazardous to Health. In D&T: wood dust (respiratory hazard from MDF), acrylic fumes (from laser cutting without extraction), solvent fumes (from lacquers and adhesives). COSHH requires adequate ventilation or an extraction system before any operation producing dust or fumes." },
  { term: "Emergency stop", definition: "A large red button (or pull-cord) that immediately cuts power to all moving parts of a machine. Location must be known before the machine is switched on. Never bypass an emergency stop." },
  { term: "Safe area / exclusion zone", definition: "The area around a machine where only the operator may stand while the machine is running. Exclusion zones prevent bystanders being struck by ejected material or moving parts." },
]} />

<Section title="Machine-by-machine safety guide" icon="⚠️" id="safety">

### Laser cutter

- PPE: safety glasses, closed-toe shoes
- Hazards: laser flash (do not look directly into the beam); fumes from cutting (always run extraction fan; never cut PVC or polycarbonate)
- Emergency: lid-open = laser stops immediately (interlock). Emergency stop cuts all power.
- Safe practice: never leave the machine unattended during a cut job

### Pillar drill

- PPE: safety glasses (no gloves — loose glove can catch in the chuck)
- Hazards: rotating chuck, ejected material if bit breaks; work piece spinning if not clamped
- Emergency: power isolator on the machine body
- Safe practice: always clamp the workpiece; lower the bit slowly; clear swarf with a brush, not fingers

### Band saw / scroll saw

- PPE: safety glasses, dust mask for MDF cutting
- Hazards: blade contact; material kickback on band saw
- Emergency: foot-operated kill switch or red button
- Safe practice: keep fingers at least 50 mm from the blade; do not cut curves tighter than the blade width

### Soldering station

- PPE: safety glasses, well-ventilated area (solder flux fumes)
- Hazards: burns (iron tip 300–450°C); flux fume inhalation
- Emergency: unplug the iron; burns station has running water adjacent
- Safe practice: return iron to stand when not in use; never hold work with bare hands while soldering

### Hand tools (chisels, knives, saws)

- PPE: safety glasses for chiselling; cut-resistant glove for holding the work when using a knife
- Hazards: cutting toward the body; slipping tools
- Safe practice: always cut away from the body; clamp or bench-stop the work before cutting

</Section>

<Activity title="Makerspace induction + quiz" time="45 min" grouping="Whole class then individual" materials={["Induction quiz (teacher issued)", "Machine identification sheet", "PPE items to handle and name"]}>

**Guided walk-around (0–20 min):**
1. Teacher leads the group to each machine. At each machine: identify the PPE required, name one hazard, locate the emergency stop.

**PPE identification (20–30 min):**
2. Pick up and correctly identify each item of PPE: when is it used? Why?

**Induction quiz (30–45 min):**
3. Complete the quiz individually: 100% required to use the makerspace. If any question is answered incorrectly, re-read the relevant section and retake until 100% is achieved.
</Activity>

<Support title="Quiz question unclear?">

**Not sure which PPE is required for a specific machine?** Ask the technician — they are the safety authority in the makerspace, not the textbook. The technician's instruction takes precedence.

**Emergency stop location not obvious?** Look for the large red circular button (often mounted on a post near the machine) or a red pull-cord. If you cannot find it before the machine is switched on, do not switch the machine on — ask the technician to show you.
</Support>
