Your coursework is submitted — take a breath. This week is for **looking back** at the
whole journey, and **looking forward**: the skills you built on this project are the
same ones the written papers test. Let's connect the two.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">

  choices, working drawings and structures all reappear in

  This review turns a year of making into exam-ready understanding.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Look back across C1–C7

Each stage taught you something: identifying a need, writing a measurable spec,
generating different ideas, developing with evidence, planning to make, realising a
product, and evaluating it. Name your **strongest** stage — and the one you found
**hardest**.

### 2. What transfers to the exams

- **Idea generation** → <ExamLink to="paper1">Paper 1 part (c)</ExamLink> (three different ideas).
- **Evaluating and justifying** → Paper 1 part (d).
- **Working drawings** → Paper 1 part (e).
- **Structures, forces and rigidity** → <ExamLink to="paper4">Paper 4 Section B</ExamLink>.

### 3. Look ahead — the revision block

Before the November exam you'll revise the **Paper 1 timed routine** and **Paper 4**
(Section A breadth + Section B Structures). Decide now where *you* most need the work.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a project skill becomes an exam mark">
1. **On the project:** you scored ideas in an **evaluation matrix** and justified your choice.
2. **In the exam:** that's exactly **Paper 1 part (d)** — evaluate three ideas, choose one, justify it.
3. **On the project:** you braced a frame with **triangulation** so it held its load.
4. **In the exam:** that's **Paper 4 Section B** — explain rigidity and triangulation in structures.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Review and aim" time="Full session + reflection" grouping="Individual" materials={["Your finished folder", "A review prompt sheet"]}>
1. **Review your project:** which stage are you **proudest** of, and which was
   **hardest**?
2. **Name one skill** you'll carry forward.
3. **Set one revision focus** for the written papers — the area you most want to
   strengthen.
</Activity>

</Support>
