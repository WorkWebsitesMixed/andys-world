trick is to build in **stages** and to **dry-fit** before you commit any glue — it's
much easier to fix a problem now than after it's stuck.

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Build in stages

Don't try to assemble everything at once. Build **sub-assemblies** — a side, a frame,
a shelf — then join those. It's easier to control quality and to keep things square.

### 2. Dry-fit first

Always **dry-fit** before fixing: slot the parts together with no glue and check they
line up, that the **fit** is right, and that the assembly sits **square**. Adjust now,
while you still can.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — dry-fit before you glue">
1. **Slot** the frame parts together without glue.
2. **Check square:** measure both diagonals — equal diagonals mean a true 90°.
3. **Check fit:** is any joint too tight or too loose? Ease or pack it now.
4. **Only then** apply glue and clamp — confident it will go together right.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Finish and fit" time="Full session (technician-managed)" grouping="Staggered stations" materials={["Remaining parts", "Your cutting list", "A try-square or ruler"]}>
1. **Finish all remaining parts.**
2. **Dry-fit a sub-assembly** and check it's square and fits.
3. **Confirm your cutting list is 100% complete** — every part made and checked.
</Activity>
