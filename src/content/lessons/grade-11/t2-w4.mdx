Last week you found problems by making. This week you **test on purpose** — with
measurements, not guesses — and you breadboard your circuit before committing it.
Honest test data is what turns a nice idea into a developed, evidence-backed design.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  Objective testing is core <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 4</ExamLink>
  (development) and sets up **Criterion 7** (testing & evaluation). The examiner
  rewards *measured* evidence — "held 5 kg", not "seemed strong".
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Objective test", definition: "A test that measures a result, rather than relying on opinion." },
  { term: "Fair test", definition: "A test where you change one thing at a time, so the result is trustworthy." },
  { term: "Load test", definition: "Measuring how much weight a structure carries before it fails." },

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Test objectively

**Measure, don't guess.** Design a **fair test**: change one thing at a time so you
know what caused the result. For a structure, the key tests are **load** (how much
weight before it fails) and **stability** (does it tip or wobble?).

### 2. Breadboard before you build

Before fixing your circuit into the product, build it on a **breadboard** — a
solderless board for testing. Confirm the switch turns the LED on and off, then
you can commit it with confidence.

<CircuitDiagram />

### 3. The iteration loop

One test is rarely the end. Run the loop: **test → modify → re-test**, recording
each result. Each turn of the loop is development evidence.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — test, modify, re-test">
1. **Test:** a cyclist's gear organiser holds 3 kg, then **twists at a joint**.
2. **Record:** note the exact load and what happened (a number, not "a bit").
3. **Modify:** add a diagonal brace (triangulation) across the joint.
4. **Re-test:** it now holds 5 kg with no twist — record the before and after.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Test and breadboard" time="50 min" grouping="Pairs (technician supports)" materials={["Weights / load rig", "Breadboard kit & multimeter", "Test-results table"]}>
1. **Run a load or stability test** and **record the data** (numbers and photos).
2. **Breadboard your circuit** (switch → resistor → LED) and confirm it works.
3. **Note the changes** your results call for.
</Activity>
