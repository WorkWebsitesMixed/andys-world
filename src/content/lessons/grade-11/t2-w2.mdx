Now you've chosen your design, it's time to **make it real — roughly and quickly.**
A cardboard model in ten minutes will teach you things a perfect drawing never
could. This is where **development** (C4) really starts.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  Modelling is the heart of <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 4</ExamLink>
  (development, 15 marks). The examiner wants to see you **trial and improve** your
  idea with evidence — not jump straight to the final make.
</Callout>

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Why model at all?

Drawing shows what something *looks* like; a model shows how it *behaves*. Models
are **cheap and quick**, so you can find problems early — the "**fail fast**"
idea. Far better a card model tips over now than your finished product in Term 3.

### 2. Types of model

- **Sketch model** — fast, from card or foam, to test shape.
- **Block model** — solid stand-in to judge size and proportion.
- **Mock-up** — a fuller version to check fit and feel.

### 3. What to test by making

Use your model to check **form, scale, ergonomics and stability** — the things a
flat drawing hides.

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a model finds a hidden problem">
1. **Make it:** a student builds a card model of their cyclist's gear organiser to scale.
2. **Load it:** they hang the helmet and shoes on it — and it **tips forward**.
3. **Diagnose:** the base footprint is too narrow for the load hung out front.
4. **Develop:** widen the base and add feet — a fix found by *making*, not drawing.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Build, break, improve" time="55 min" grouping="Modelling-buddy pairs" materials={["Card & foam board", "Glue gun, cutting mat", "Any found materials"]}>
1. **Build a quick model** of your structure, roughly to scale.
2. **Find at least two problems** it reveals (does it tip? flex? feel awkward?).
3. **Sketch a fix** for each problem on your development page.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? A starting kit + a reframe">
Grab a **pre-cut card kit** to get going fast — don't worry about neatness, this
is a thinking tool.

**Reframe a 'failed' model:** *"My model showed me **………**, so I will **………**."*
Every flaw you find is a development mark earned.
</Support>

<Section title="Homework & extension" icon="🏠" id="homework">

**Homework:** photograph your model and **annotate the problems** you found.

<Extension title="Go deeper">
Make a **second version** of one feature (say, two different bases) and compare —
which performs better, and how do you know?
</Extension>
