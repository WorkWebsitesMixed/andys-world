Term 3 is making term. Everything from Terms 1 and 2 — the research, the design, the development — is now the blueprint. Your job is to manufacture a product that matches it. Today you check your production plan is specific enough to guide 9 sessions of making, and you warm up Paper 4 knowledge so it does not evaporate while your hands are busy in the makerspace.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  Manufacturing evidence is assessed in <ExamLink to="project">Component 2</ExamLink> criterion **C6 (making, 30 marks)** — the single largest criterion in the entire project. Thirty marks depend on what you make and how you record making it. A good production plan is not just a folder requirement: it is how you use your makerspace time without wasting a session.
</Callout>

  { term: "C6 making evidence", definition: "Photographs of the product at each stage of manufacture, with brief notes on the process, tools used, and any problems encountered. C6 marks come from evidence of quality making — not from the finished product alone." },

<Section title="Finalising your production plan" icon="📋" id="plan">

### From term plan to session plan

Your T2 W9 production plan listed steps in order. Now make it session-specific:

- Which steps happen in T3 W2?
- Which steps happen in T3 W3?
- Where is the 3D print — does it need to start before session 2 to be ready for assembly in session 4?
- Where are the finish coats, and how do they fit around the cure time?

Write a session-by-session plan: T3 W2 through T3 W9. Each session gets a list of 2–4 steps and a time estimate. Total planned time across all sessions should not exceed 810 minutes (9 × 90 min).

### Identifying your critical path

Find the step that cannot be rushed and cannot be skipped: this is your critical path bottleneck. Common examples:

- 3D print: 90 min machine time, cannot be done at the bench. Start it in W2 or it delays assembly.
- Finish coat 1 + 2: 12 h minimum dry time between coats. Must be scheduled at least two sessions apart.
- Laser cut: requires machine booking — confirm the machine is available for W2 before planning it for W2.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Session plan for a cyclist gear organiser">

| Session | Steps | Time estimate |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| T3 W2 | Laser cut panels; 3D print bracket (set at start of session, collect next session); sand panels | 80 min |
| T3 W3 | Collect 3D print; mark out and cut any hand-tool parts; dry-assemble frame to check fit | 75 min |
| T3 W4 | Glue and assemble main frame; cut peg holes; fit pegs | 70 min |
| T3 W5 | Wire circuit: solder switch, resistor, LED; test; mount in bracket | 80 min |
| T3 W6 | Apply finish coat 1; photograph progress for C6 | 40 min |
| T3 W7 | Apply finish coat 2; photograph progress for C6 | 40 min |
| T3 W8 | Final assembly and check; product photography | 60 min |
| T3 W9 | Testing and evaluation against spec; complete folder | 90 min |
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Session plan + Section A warm-up" time="90 min" grouping="Individual (teacher reviews session plans)" materials={["T2 W9 production plan", "Session plan template", "Section A warm-up card (teacher issued)"]}>

**Session plan (0–40 min):**
1. Open your T2 W9 production plan. Read through it. Does it have enough detail to guide a real session — or is it too vague?
2. Using the session plan format above, assign each step to a specific T3 session.
3. Identify your critical path bottleneck. Write it at the top of the session plan: "Critical path: ___. Starting in session ___."
4. Share the plan with the teacher for a 2-minute review.

**Folder check (40–60 min):**
5. Check that C3, C4, and C5 are in order. If anything is missing, add it now — the folder is submitted with the product at the end of T3.

**Section A warm-up (60–90 min):**
6. Teacher issues a Section A warm-up card: 10 short questions from Paper 4 topics.
7. Answer in silence, 20 minutes.
8. Mark against the key sheet. Note any topic where you score fewer than 3/10 — that topic needs a bonus session or home revision.
</Activity>

<Support title="Session plan too vague?">

**Every session says 'making'?** That is too vague. Break each session into at least two specific steps: "Mark out and drill peg holes (30 min)" and "Sand and clean up edges (20 min)" is specific. "Make the product" is not.

**Can't fit everything into 9 sessions?** Look for steps that can be parallelised (3D print running while you hand-finish another part) and steps that can be shortened (dry assembly can be 15 min, not 60 min if all parts fit first time). If it still does not fit, you may need to simplify one design feature — do this now, not in Week 6.

**Section A score very low?** That is useful data. Your two lowest-scoring topics become your Bonus session priorities. Do not try to revise all topics at once.
</Support>

<Section title="Homework" icon="🏠" id="homework">

**Home task:** Review the Section A warm-up topics where you scored lowest. Revise one of those topics for 20 minutes using the Collins textbook. Write five key facts about that topic in your own words and put them in your folder revision section.

<Extension title="Visualise Week 2">
Before the next session: close your eyes and run through the entire T3 W2 plan in your head. What tool will you use for the first operation? Where is the machine? What safety check do you do before using it? Is the material available? Walk through every step mentally — this kind of pre-mortum thinking catches problems before they happen in the makerspace.
</Extension>
