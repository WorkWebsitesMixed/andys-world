<Section title="Structures: deep dive" icon="🏗️" id="structures">

### The five force types — exam-ready definitions

| Force type | Definition | Recognise it by |
|---|---|---|
| Tension | Force that pulls a member apart along its length | Member being stretched; cable or tie rod |
| Compression | Force that pushes a member together along its length | Member being shortened; column, strut |
| Bending | Force that causes curvature — combines tension on one face and compression on the other | Horizontal beam under vertical load |
| Shear | Force that causes one layer to slide relative to the adjacent layer | Bolt through two plates being pulled apart |
| Torsion | Twisting force — causes rotation about the member's long axis | A shaft being twisted; a screw being turned |

### FoS calculation

FoS = Ultimate load / Working load

A FoS of 2.0 means the structure can support twice the load it is designed for before failing.

**Given FoS and working load — find ultimate load:**
Ultimate load = FoS x Working load

**Given ultimate load and working load — find FoS:**
FoS = Ultimate load / Working load

### Beam reactions

For a simply supported beam with a single point load:
- RA = W x (distance from load to B) / span
- RB = W x (distance from load to A) / span
- Check: RA + RB must equal W.

Where W is the load, A is the left support, B is the right support, span is the distance A to B.

### Stress calculation

Stress = Force / Area

Where force is in N and area is in mm². Result is in N/mm² (= MPa).

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Three structures calculations in sequence">

**Scenario:** A cantilever shelf bracket carries a 4 kg load at its tip. The bracket arm is 200 mm long. The cross-section is 40 mm x 8 mm solid mild steel. FoS required: 2.5. Yield strength of mild steel: 250 MPa.

**Step 1 — Force from load:**
W = mass x g = 4 x 10 = 40 N

**Step 2 — Stress at the bracket root:**
Stress = Force / Area = 40 / (40 x 8) = 40 / 320 = 0.125 N/mm²

**Step 3 — Compare to safe working stress:**
Safe working stress = Yield strength / FoS = 250 / 2.5 = 100 MPa

**Step 4 — Is the bracket safe?**
0.125 MPa much less than 100 MPa — yes, massively over-designed. The bracket could carry: 100 MPa x 320 mm² = 32,000 N = 3,200 kg. Designed for 4 kg.

**Conclusion:** The stress calculation shows the bracket is structurally safe with an enormous safety margin, suggesting the material and cross-section are selected for cost and manufacturing convenience, not structural necessity.
</WorkedExample>

<Support title="Structures revision reminders">

**Force vs stress:** Force is in N (newtons). Stress is in N/mm² (pascals, or MPa). Stress = Force / Area. You need the cross-sectional area in mm² to calculate stress.

**FoS direction:** FoS is always greater than 1.0 for a safe design. FoS = Ultimate / Working. If you calculate a FoS less than 1.0, you have divided the wrong way round.

**Beam reactions check:** Always verify that RA + RB = W. If the reactions do not add up to the total load, there is an arithmetic error somewhere.
</Support>
