This is a full making week: you cut and print most of your parts. The skill that
separates a good product from a frustrating one is **accuracy** — every part made to
your drawing, every part checked before you move on.

<Callout type="exam" title="Why this matters" id="why">
  This is the heart of <ExamLink to="project">Criterion 6</ExamLink> (product
  realisation). Marks come from parts made **to a good standard, to your drawing** —
  so check every one against your cutting list.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Accuracy", definition: "Making each part to the exact size on your working drawing." },
  { term: "Cutting list", definition: "Your list of every part, its material, size and quantity — check parts against it." },
  { term: "Failed cut/print", definition: "A part that comes out wrong — usually a kerf, scale or orientation issue to fix." },

<Section title="The lesson" icon="📋" id="lesson">

### 1. Make it accurate

Work to your **working drawing** dimensions, and **QC every part against your cutting
list** as it comes off the machine. A part that's 2 mm out now becomes a joint that
won't fit later.

### 2. When a part comes out wrong

It happens. Don't bin it in frustration — diagnose it. Usually it's **kerf** (the
laser's cut width), the **scale** of the file, or the **orientation** of a print.
Adjust the file and re-run.

### 3. Marking out by hand

For any hand-worked parts, **mark out** carefully first — measure twice, cut once.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Worked example — a part comes out undersized">
1. **Measure** the finished part against the cutting list — it's 2 mm too small.
2. **Diagnose:** the file didn't allow for **kerf**, so the laser removed extra.
3. **Correct** the file (add the kerf allowance) — and double-check the scale.
4. **Re-cut**, then QC again. Right this time? Move on.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Make and check your parts" time="Full session (technician-managed rotation)" grouping="Staggered stations" materials={["Your production plan & cut files", "Cutting list", "QC log"]}>
1. **Manufacture your parts** following your plan.
2. **QC each part** against the cutting list and your drawing.
3. **Log progress** and flag any parts you need to re-make.
</Activity>

<Support title="Need a hand? Spares and priority">
**Pre-cut spares** of common parts are available so a failed cut doesn't stop you.
If you're behind, you get **technician priority** at the machines — ask early, not at
the end.
</Support>
