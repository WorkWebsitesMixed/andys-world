<Section title="Exam day strategy" icon="💻" id="strategy">

### Before the paper starts (0–5 min)

Your teacher will confirm:
- File naming convention for each application (Word, Access, PowerPoint).
- Save locations.
- Exam rules: no peer help; raise hand for technical issues only.

### During the paper: time allocation

Paper 2 is 2.5 hours (150 minutes). Rough guide — not a strict rule:
- Document production (Word): **~55–65 min**
- Database (Access): **~45–55 min**
- Presentation (PowerPoint): **~25–35 min**

Adjust based on the mark allocation visible in the question paper. Don't spend 70 minutes on Word and leave 15 for Access.

### The two most time-critical decisions

1. **Mail merge:** if you're stuck on the wizard steps after 5 minutes, skip the merge and complete every other Word task first. Come back to it with remaining time. Don't let mail merge sink the entire Word section.
2. **Access query logic:** re-read the question for AND/OR before entering any criteria. One wrong row choice and the result set is completely wrong.

### After the paper: the debrief

Self-mark against the mark scheme. Annotate green / red / ★. Write your **top 3 starred items** on a deduction card and hand it to your teacher along with your Week 3 card. Your teacher now has both papers covered and will plan Week 7 around the most common class-wide gaps.

</Section>

<Section title="Theory: Paper 1 — Email, internet, intranet & environmental impact" icon="📖" id="theory">

### Email: components and risks

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| To | Primary recipient — they know who else received it |
| CC (carbon copy) | Copied recipients — all recipients see who is in CC |
| BCC (blind carbon copy) | Copied secretly — other recipients don't know they're included |
| Attachment | A file sent with the email — not embedded in the body |

**Risk of attachments:** malware can be embedded in attachment files. Opening an attachment from an unknown sender is a common infection route.

### Identifying a phishing email

A phishing email is designed to trick you into revealing credentials. Signs:
- Generic greeting ("Dear Customer" instead of your name).
- Urgent language ("Your account will be closed in 24 hours").
- A link whose domain doesn't match the claimed sender.
- Mismatched sender address (looks official, but check the full address).

### Internet vs intranet

| | Internet | Intranet |
|---|---|---|
| Access | Public — anyone | Restricted — authorised users only |
| Content | Global; all websites | Internal company resources |
| Purpose | General information and communication | Internal collaboration and resource sharing |

### Environmental impact of ICT

| Problem | Cause | Countermeasure |
|---|---|---|
| Energy consumption | Data centres run 24/7; server cooling | Renewable energy for data centres; energy-saving modes |
| E-waste | Toxic materials in old devices | Manufacturer recycling programmes; extending device lifespan |
| Carbon footprint | Shipping devices; manufacturing | Cloud computing reduces physical hardware; virtualisation |

**Weak-answer trap:** "ICT is bad for the environment because it uses electricity." → *"Large-scale data centres consume significant electrical energy continuously; if this energy comes from fossil fuels it contributes to greenhouse gas emissions. Countermeasures include powering data centres with renewable energy and using virtualisation to reduce the number of physical servers required."*

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Weak answer → improved answer (intranet vs internet)">
**Question:** "A company uses an intranet. State two differences between a company intranet and the internet."

Weak: *"The intranet is private and the internet is public."* (1 mark — only one difference, stated vaguely)

Improved:
1. *"The intranet can only be accessed by authorised employees of the company; the internet can be accessed by anyone."*
2. *"The intranet contains internal company documents and resources; the internet contains publicly available information from organisations worldwide."*

Each improved answer gives a **specific difference** with enough detail for the examiner to award the mark.
</WorkedExample>

<Support title="If you're running out of time">
Prioritise in this order:
1. Complete any task you're in the middle of — a half-done task earns fewer marks than a finished one.
2. Start any untouched tasks — even a correct first step earns something.
3. Check filenames and save formats — these are free marks that cost only seconds to verify.
</Support>
