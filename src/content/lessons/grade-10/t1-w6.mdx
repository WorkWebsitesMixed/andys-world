<Section title="Specification review: strengthening weak points" icon="📝" id="spec-review">

### The two-test checklist

For each specification point, apply two tests:

1. **Measurable test:** Can I confirm whether the finished product meets this point using a ruler, stopwatch, load, or observation? If not, rewrite it.
2. **Justification test:** Can I trace this point back to the brief constraint, the user need, or a safety rule? If not, it may not belong in the specification.

### Common weak points and how to fix them

| Weak point | Problem | Stronger version |
|---|---|---|
| "The door must open quickly" | Not measurable — how quickly? | "The door must fully open within 5 seconds of the thermistor reaching 40°C" |
| "The mechanism must be strong" | 'Strong' is generic and untested | "The crank arm must support a minimum lateral load of 2 N without bending" |
| "Must look good" | Aesthetic, not functional | Remove from spec or rewrite as "the door panel surface must be smooth to the touch with no exposed fixings" |
| "Must be safe" | Everything must be safe | Specify the hazard: "All exposed edges on the door panel must be sanded to 240 grit and finished to eliminate sharp edges" |

### Development sketches: showing how it will be built

A development sketch develops the *chosen* mechanism design. Unlike idea generation (three different concepts), a development sketch focuses on one idea and shows:
- **Materials** for each individual part (e.g., "mild steel 3 mm for crank arm")
- **How parts join** (e.g., "M4 bolt through crank arm to motor shaft hub")
- **Where components sit** inside the building façade (motor position, clearances)

You need two development sketches showing alternative *construction* approaches for the same mechanism — not two different mechanisms.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Converting a vague spec point and drawing a development sketch">
**Vague spec point:** "The motor must fit inside the building."

**Stronger version:** "The motor and crank arm assembly must fit within a 40 × 40 × 60 mm envelope inside the pre-made façade, because the façade interior depth is 65 mm."

Now measurable (40 × 40 × 60 mm), now justified (façade depth constraint from the brief).

**Development sketch example — two approaches to attaching the crank arm to the motor shaft:**
- **Option A:** Set-screw collar — a metal hub with a grub screw that clamps onto the motor shaft. Annotation: "mild steel hub — grub screw allows adjustment of crank position — removable for maintenance."
- **Option B:** Press-fit hub — plastic hub pushed directly onto the D-shaped motor shaft. Annotation: "ABS plastic hub — D-flat prevents rotation — permanent joint, lower cost than machined steel."
</WorkedExample>

<Section title="Bonus session: Moment 1 — Paper 1 (a)+(b) graded test" icon="📝" id="m1-test">

### What M1 assesses

| Part | What you must produce | Marks available |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | Four function points for the given product — specific, no generic words | 4 marks |
| (b) | Two clearly different annotated design methods — each with material, join, and function annotations | 8+ marks |

### Run sheet for M1

- **0–5 min:** Read the context carefully. Underline key words. Do not start writing yet.
- **5–11 min:** Write part (a) — four function points. Allow 90 seconds per point.
- **11–40 min:** Part (b) — sketch method 1 (annotated), then method 2 (genuinely different from method 1, annotated). Allocate 12 minutes per sketch.
- **40–45 min:** Review: check each annotation against the checklist. If you have time, add one more annotation per sketch.

### If you freeze on a sketch

Move to the text annotations first. Write four annotation notes on the page (even before the sketch is detailed). Then add the sketch to match. This gets marks on the page and breaks the blank-page freeze.

</Section>

<Activity title="Specification review and development sketches" time="90 min (W session)" grouping="Individual + peer review" materials={["Project folder with draft specification", "A3 paper for development sketches", "Pencil and ruler"]}>
1. **Peer-review your specification:** Swap with a partner. For each point: does it pass the measurable test? Does it pass the justification test? Write one comment per weak point — suggest a specific fix, not just "needs improvement."
2. **Finalise your specification** to at least six points. Add at least one safety point and one sustainability point if not already present.
3. **Produce two development sketches** of the mechanism construction. Each sketch shows a different approach to building or mounting one part (not two different mechanisms). Annotate each with materials, join types, and approximate dimensions.
</Activity>

<Support title="Scaffolds for specification and development sketches">
**Specification checklist:** Is each point measurable? Is each justified? Do you have a safety point? A sustainability point? Six or more total?

**Development sketch template:** Two empty boxes with prompt labels — "Component/part," "Material," "Join method," "Dimension approx." Use these as your annotation guide inside each box.

**M1 preparation:** Review your Week 2 function-point formula and Week 5 annotation checklist before the bonus session. These two tools are all you need.
</Support>
