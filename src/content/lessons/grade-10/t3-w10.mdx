Grade 10 was a preparation year. Everything you have done — the Firefighter Barbie system, the Desk Organizer, the Water Bottle analysis — was practice for Grade 11 IGCSE Component 2 and the two exam papers. Today you map what you practised to what Grade 11 will require, and you set three specific targets.

</Callout>

<Section title="What Grade 10 gave you — mapped to Grade 11" icon="🗺️" id="readiness">

| IGCSE criterion | Grade 10 practice | Grade 11 requirement |
|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| C1 — Brief | T1 (Firefighter Barbie), T2 (Desk Organizer), T3 (Redesign brief) | Student chooses own brief — no given project |
| C2 — Specification | Three practice specs, each graded (T1, T2, T3) | Full specification from own brief; assessed by examiner |
| C3 — Idea generation | Paper 1 parts (c)(d)(e) every term; T1 mechanism ideas | Three or more developed ideas with full annotation |
| C4 — Development | Development sketches (T2), OnShape CAD (T2, T3), modelling | Model or prototype evidence; testing against spec |
| C5 — Planning + working drawings | Manufacturing plan (T3), engineering drawing (T2) | Full working drawings in correct projection |
| C6 — Making | Desk Organizer (T2) | Full product realised in a range of materials and processes |
| C7 — Testing + evaluation | T1 Firefighter Barbie test; T2 Desk Organizer eval; T3 redesign eval | Full specification test table + written evaluation |
| Paper 1 | Parts (a)(b) T1 M1; (c)(d)(e) T2 M1; (f)(g) T2 W10 | Complete 90-minute Paper 1 |
| Paper 3 | Partial Section A mock (T2 M3); full mock (T3 M3) | Complete 90-minute Paper 3 |

### What Grade 11 changes

- **You choose your own brief.** There is no given project — the brief comes from a context you identify.
- **C6 requires sustained making.** The Desk Organizer was one term; the IGCSE project is a full year.
- **All seven criteria must be addressed.** Missing a criterion means the examiner cannot mark it.
- **Paper 1 and Paper 3 are sat in the same exam week.** Both require sustained preparation throughout the year.

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Setting a specific Grade 11 target">

**Vague target:** "Improve my sketching."

**Specific target:** "Produce one A4 page of annotated isometric sketches each week in Term 1, using the crating technique for all mechanism components. By the end of T1 W5, I should be able to sketch a complete slider-crank mechanism freehand in under 10 minutes with correct annotation."

Notice: the specific target names the skill (isometric crating), the practice method (one A4 page per week), the timeline (by T1 W5), and the measurable outcome (under 10 minutes, correct annotation). It can be checked at T1 W5.
</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Year review and G11 preparation" time="90 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["Readiness map template", "All three term folders", "Target card (collected by teacher at end)"]}>

**Readiness map (0–45 min):**
1. Fill in the readiness map: for each row (C1–C7, Paper 1, Paper 3), tick the column that best describes your current state:
   - Fully practised — I could do this independently right now
   - Partially practised — I have started this but need more experience
   - Not yet encountered — I have not done this at all
2. Identify the two criteria/paper parts where you are least ready. Write them at the bottom of the map.

**Three Grade 11 targets (45–60 min):**
3. Write three Grade 11 targets — one each for: (a) Component 2 (choose one criterion), (b) Paper 1 (choose one part), (c) Paper 3 (Section A or Section B).
4. Each target must be specific: name the skill, the practice method, and a way to know when it is achieved.

**Portfolio review + progress paragraph (60–80 min):**
5. Open all three term folders. Select one piece from each term — the one that best represents your progress as a designer. Mark with a sticky note.
6. Write one sentence for each piece explaining why you chose it.
7. Write a 100-word progress paragraph: compare your confidence with design communication, materials knowledge, and manufacturing knowledge at the start vs the end of this year.

**Share + collect (80–90 min):**
8. Share one target with a partner — ask them to hold you to it in G11 T1 W1.
9. Teacher collects the target card.
</Activity>

<Support title="If the readiness map shows many 'not yet encountered' ticks">

This is expected. Grade 10 is the bridge year — it is designed to expose you to all parts of the IGCSE without requiring full mastery. The readiness map shows where Grade 11 must focus, not where Grade 10 failed. The most important insight is: which criterion needs the most work in Grade 11, and which of your three targets addresses it?
</Support>
