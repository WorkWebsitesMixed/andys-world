The manufacturing plan is the most complex document in the M2 package. Every row must be complete: no blank cells. A plan with gaps is not a plan — it is a sketch. Today you complete it and begin the most focused Section A revision session of the year.

<Callout type="exam" title="Graded Moment 2 — 30% of term" id="graded">
  **M2 (30%) is collected at the start of Week 9.** The package includes: redesign brief + specification + design sketches + manufacturing plan (all components, all columns, QC + H&S). Teacher and drawing colleague co-mark together. Check every page before next session.
</Callout>

<KeyTerms terms={[
  { term: "Quality control checkpoint placement", definition: "QC checkpoints are placed at critical steps in the sequence — after operations where a defect would be expensive to fix downstream. For a bottle cap: wall thickness after moulding (before threading — a thin wall cannot hold the thread); thread dimension after tooling (before assembly — a wrong thread will not fit the bottle); cap torque before labelling (a loose cap will leak in use)." },
  { term: "Go/no-go gauge", definition: "A gauging tool that checks whether a dimension falls within tolerance limits without measuring the exact value. The GO end of the gauge (maximum acceptable size) must fit; the NO-GO end (minimum acceptable + tolerance) must not fit. Rapid, non-destructive, usable by an unskilled operator on a production line." },
  { term: "Section A timing strategy", definition: "Paper 3 Section A is approximately 40 marks. Total time: allow 1–1.5 min per mark. Common trap: spending 6 minutes on a 2-mark question, leaving insufficient time for a 6-mark question. Write the main point first; add detail only if time allows. Never leave a sub-part blank — a material name alone earns 1 mark even without a full explanation." },
  { term: "Tolerance notation", definition: "Written as: target value ± allowable deviation. Example: 28.0 ± 0.1 mm. This means the acceptable range is 27.9 mm to 28.1 mm. In a manufacturing plan, every QC checkpoint must state the tolerance — otherwise the checkpoint cannot distinguish a passing from a failing measurement." },
]} />

<Section title="Quality control techniques" icon="✅" id="qc">

### Common QC methods for water bottle production

| Step checked | QC method | Tolerance (example) | Action on failure |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Bottle wall thickness | Ultrasonic thickness gauge | 0.20 ± 0.05 mm | Reject; raw material returned |
| Cap thread diameter | Go/no-go gauge | 28.0 ± 0.1 mm | Reject; remelt |
| Cap opening torque | Torque meter | 0.5–1.5 N·m | Adjust mould temperature |
| Bottle leak test | Fill and invert at 2 bar, 30 s | Zero leakage | Reject batch; check sealing station |
| Label position | Machine vision system | ± 2 mm from target | Auto-reject; recalibrate |

### Health and safety at key manufacturing steps

| Process | Specific hazard | PPE / precaution |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Injection moulding | Molten PP at 230°C | Heat-resistant gloves + face shield |
| Blow moulding | Compressed air (up to 40 bar) | Interlocked guards; no access during cycle |
| CNC machining | Moving cutters; swarf | Machine guards + emergency stop; no loose clothing |
| Chemical finishing (anodising) | Acid bath (sulphuric acid) | Acid-resistant apron, gloves, face shield; emergency shower |
| UV printing | UV lamp exposure | UV-blocking eyewear; enclosed print station |

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="Complete plan row — PP bottle cap">

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Component | Bottle cap |
| Material | Polypropylene (PP), food grade, white |
| Commercial process | Injection moulding |
| Justification | PP is injection mouldable; the cap requires complex internal thread geometry and high dimensional precision at the thread diameter — only injection moulding can produce this geometry with sufficient repeatability at 2 million units/year. |
| Sequence | 1. PP granules loaded into hopper. 2. Rotating screw melts and pushes PP toward nozzle (~230°C). 3. Molten PP injected under pressure into cooled steel mould. 4. Mould cool water channels reduce PP to ~50°C. 5. Mould opens; ejector pins release cap. 6. Cap inspected: thread diameter go/no-go gauge. |
| H&S | Molten PP at 230°C — heat-resistant gloves and face shield worn when clearing blockages; machine guards closed before injection cycle starts. |
| QC | Thread diameter: go/no-go gauge, tolerance 28.0 ± 0.1 mm. Checked every 500 units. Units outside tolerance rejected and remelted. |

</WorkedExample>

<Activity title="Complete the plan + peer check + Section A drill" time="90 min" grouping="Individual" materials={["Manufacturing plan in progress", "QC template", "Paper 3 Section A drill sheet (10 items, all year content)", "Revision card"]}>

**Complete the plan (0–60 min):**
1. Complete any unfinished manufacturing plan rows — every column must have content.
2. Add QC checkpoints to at least two steps: what is checked, method, tolerance, action on failure.
3. Add H&S notes to at least two manufacturing steps: specific hazard + specific precaution.

**Peer check (60–70 min):**
4. Swap plans with a partner. Circle every blank cell. Return with a written note: "Blank cells found in: ___."
5. Fill any blanks identified.

**Paper 3 Section A timed drill (70–90 min):**
6. 10 items across all year content: material properties (4), joining (2), commercial processes (3), sustainability (1).
7. Allow approximately 1.5 min per mark.
8. Self-mark. Write two revision targets on your revision card: "Before M3, I need to review: (1) ___ (2) ___."
</Activity>

<Support title="If you cannot answer a drill question">

Write the material or process name anyway — even a named material without a full explanation earns 1 mark. Then flag it on your revision card. Do not leave the question blank. Partial credit on a question you are unsure of is better than zero from a blank.
</Support>
