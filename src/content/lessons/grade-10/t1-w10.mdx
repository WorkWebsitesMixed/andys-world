<Section title="Installation and testing" icon="🔧" id="installation">

### Integrating the mechanism and circuit

Before connecting everything, check:
1. **Motor is securely mounted** — use the pre-drilled motor-mount holes in the façade. A motor that moves will misalign the crank arm.
2. **Crank arm is correctly positioned** — the door should be closed when the crank arm is at its natural rest position.
3. **Connecting rod is attached** so the door pivot arm can move freely through its full arc without binding.
4. **Circuit connections** — all leads securely in breadboard, no loose ends. Thermistor lead away from the motor (vibration can dislodge it).

### Testing the system: what "working" means

The Firefighter Barbie system **works** when:
- The motor does **not** activate at room temperature.
- The motor **does** activate when the thermistor is warmed by hand or breath (approximately 35–40°C).
- The door opens **measurably** — at least the angle stated in your specification (e.g., 60°).
- The door returns toward closed when the thermistor cools.

### Troubleshooting sequence

If the motor does not activate when the thermistor is warmed:
1. Check battery voltage — is it above 7 V? (Use a voltmeter or swap battery.)
2. Check all circuit connections are secure.
3. Check the transistor orientation — base, collector, emitter in the right holes.
4. Try warming the thermistor more directly (breathe on it or hold it briefly against your palm).

If the motor activates but the door doesn't move:
1. Check the crank arm is securely attached to the motor shaft — does it slip?
2. Check the connecting rod attachment to the door pivot — is there a firm mechanical link?

### Test evidence page

After testing, produce a test evidence page in your folder:
- Annotated photograph or sketch of the installed system (label each component's position).
- Results table: temperature at activation, door angle achieved, time to open.
- Conclusion: does the system meet the four specification points you can test today?

</Section>

<WorkedExample title="What a results table looks like">
| Test | Method | Result | Specification met? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Motor activates at elevated temperature | Warm thermistor by hand | Motor activated after approximately 10 seconds | Yes |
| Door opens at least 60° | Protractor against door hinge | Door opened 68° | Yes |
| Motor does not activate at room temperature | Leave 5 minutes undisturbed | Motor remained off | Yes |
| Door returns toward closed when thermistor cools | Remove hand, wait 30 seconds | Door returned to 15° (not fully closed) | Partially — investigate spring return |

Note that "partially met" is an honest, valid result. A test that always passes regardless of performance is not a real test.
</WorkedExample>

<Section title="Exam practice — Paper 1 part (c)" icon="📝" id="paper1-c">

### What part (c) asks

Part (c) of Paper 1 gives you a brief for a new product and asks you to sketch **three separate, annotated design ideas**. Each idea must be:
- **Conceptually different** from the others — not the same mechanism/form drawn three times.
- **Annotated** for material, join, and function (at least one annotation per sketch).
- **Labelled** with the motion type at key components (if a mechanism).

Examiners specifically look for evidence that the three ideas are genuinely different approaches, not the same concept restyled.

### Time management in part (c)

Allow yourself **3–4 minutes per idea**: 2 minutes to sketch, 1–2 minutes to annotate. Do not spend all your time on the first idea and leave the other two as bare outlines.

### Three ideas for a new scenario

This session: practise part (c) for a given mechanism context (a sliding bin lid, a pop-up display stand, or similar). Sketch three different mechanisms:
- Idea 1: A slider-crank.
- Idea 2: A pear cam with follower.
- Idea 3: A rack-and-pinion (or lever linkage).

Each labelled with: motion type, key part names, one material note, and one function note.

</Section>

<Support title="Installation and testing guide">
**Pre-drilled motor-mount holes:** The façade has holes positioned for the motor. Use the screws in the kit bag — do not improvise. A motor that moves will misalign the whole mechanism.

**Door angle measurement:** Use a protractor or angle-measuring app. Place one arm along the closed-door position and measure the angle to the fully open position.

**Part (c) idea-per-box template:** If blank-page anxiety is a problem, use the three-box template — one box per idea. Having the box boundary reduces the tendency to over-draw idea 1 and run out of space.
</Support>
